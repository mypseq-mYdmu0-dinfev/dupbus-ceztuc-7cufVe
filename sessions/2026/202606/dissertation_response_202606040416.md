3. C0 —— Perspective / Framing Shift (§1 Introduction)

- 3.1. What she says: the current framing, whereby CYC studies TPS as a challenge observed "across its client engagements", does not satisfy the module requirement of addressing a management challenge "within your work organisation". Her proposed reframe: study TPS's influence on workplace dynamics in Australian KBFs to inform CYC's own organisational design and people management strategies.
- 3.2. Assessment: Lu is correct, and this is the highest-priority comment in the batch. Reading A1R §1 as a neutral reviewer, CYC is cast as a KM consultancy studying clients' problems, with the research telos explicitly framed as an "advisory framework supporting KBF clients" (final sentence of §1). The module requirement requires practitioner inquiry into one's own organisation; the current text reads closer to a consulting engagement proposal.
- 3.3. Critically, the reframe is low-disruption to the research substance. The KM governance angle remains intact —— it just changes what the findings are for. Instead of "CYC studies TPS to advise clients", the logic becomes: "CYC is itself a newly established KBF; as it grows, it faces the same TPS dynamics documented in its KBF client network; this study informs CYC's own people management and organisational design decisions before those dynamics take root." The intellectual content of §1–§3 does not change; the framing of purpose and beneficiary does.
- 3.4. "Australian KBFs" scope: adding this geographic qualifier to the aim is consistent with the participant pool (AD08.4 shows predominantly Australian contacts), the §2.4 TPS in AU KBF subsection already present, and the AHRI/CIPD/Billan practitioner evidence in §1. It strengthens local practitioner relevance and satisfies Lu's explicit language.
- 3.5. "Define KBF and TPS before using them": reading A1R, both are in fact introduced with abbreviation expansion at first use and TPS has a formal definition in para 2 of §1. What Lu likely means is that under the new aim's framing, these terms need a more prominent narrative introduction (as the challenge CYC itself faces) rather than being tucked into parentheses. This will be addressed naturally when §1 para 2 is rewritten.
- 3.6. Recommendation: adopt in full. Two specific passages require rewriting; a third requires one sentence change.
  - 3.6.1. §1 para 2 (CYC introduction): current text positions CYC as a KM consultant studying clients; rewrite to position CYC as a newly established KBF facing TPS dynamics internally. Suggested new opening sentences:

The imperative to understand how Tall Poppy Syndrome (TPS) operates in Australian KBFs arises directly from CYC's need to design its own people management and knowledge governance practices from the outset, before the social dynamics that erode voluntary knowledge contribution can take hold. TPS, theorised by Feather (1989, 1994) as the social disposition whereby high-performing individuals face peer marginalisation in response to their standout performance, is a governance-critical phenomenon for any KBF...

    The remainder of para 2 (mechanism, KM implications) carries forward unchanged.

- 7.3. Proposed restructure of §3:
  - 7.3.1. New §3.1 "Research Philosophy": constructivist ontology (TPS dynamics are socially constructed through peer perception, attribution, and group behaviour; no single objective reality to measure) + interpretivist epistemology (knowledge arises from meaning-making within context; statistical regularity cannot capture the situationally contingent nature of TPS experience; consistent with the inductive approach and Saunders et al., 2023 citation already in §3). Both are already implicit in the current §3.1 language; they need to be surfaced as explicit, named positions with brief justification.
  - 7.3.2. New §3.2 "Research Approach": inductive reasoning (patterns emerge from participant data rather than hypothesis testing); qualitative methodology (semi-structured interview data cannot be quantified without losing the contextual richness that makes TPS dynamics observable at peer level). The "confirmation bias" language already in §3.1 is precisely the right content for this subsection.
  - 7.3.3. New §3.3 "Research Method": the explanatory multi-case study justification currently in §3.1 (Yin 2003; Zainal 2007; replication logic) moves here intact; this is already the strongest-written passage in §3.
