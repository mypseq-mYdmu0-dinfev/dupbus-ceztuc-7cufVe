### Outcome Summary
The debate ran 15–16 turns per debater across three positions (A=remote, B=office, C=structured hybrid). No position conceded; all three remain internally consistent at close. However, the logical terrain shifted substantially:

- **A (remote)** held its core position throughout, strongest on individual-output evidence (Ctrip, patent examiner, McKinsey 40% independent-task taxonomy) and burnout/attrition costs of office mandates.
- **B (office)** progressively narrowed from "office is universally superior" to "office is optimal where commute and talent constraints are absent" (B036/B037), then to "office is the lower-risk default in the absence of median-firm RCTs" (B043/B045). B's most durable points: weak-tie atrophy under fully-remote (Bernstein), ambient awareness via sociometric data (Waber 2010/2022), and tacit knowledge irreversibility (Nonaka/Takeuchi, B047).
- **C (structured hybrid)** entered in turn 7 (0344) and was the most effective debater structurally, turning B's concessions into C's own thesis and achieving the clearest logical closure: C027 used B's own B045 concession (weak-tie atrophy reversible; institutional knowledge loss not) to argue that hybrid anchor days address the irreversibility risk without a full-office mandate.

### Key Unresolved Nodes (for MA's judgement)
1. **Weak-tie dose calibration**: Waber 2022 shows 2–3 in-person days = within 1 SD of full office on ambient awareness. B argues "within 1 SD" is not parity; C accepts the dose-responsiveness. The empirical gap is whether 2–3 days is sufficient across all subgroups or only in aggregate.
2. **Median-firm implementation**: B's strongest residual claim — the median firm cannot enforce hybrid anchor-day discipline, making office-default safer. This remains unresolved: CIPD 74% implementation success (C017) is contested by B as insufficient.
3. **Attrition temporality**: B claims RTO attrition is transitional (post-pandemic expectation shock); A/SHRM data show 24-month persistence. MA may wish to weigh which temporal framing is more credible.
4. **Tacit vs explicit knowledge**: B047 (Nonaka/Takeuchi socialisation) vs A048 (GitLab handbook) is the sharpest unresolved empirical dispute — whether codified explicit knowledge can substitute for physical socialisation in knowledge transfer.
