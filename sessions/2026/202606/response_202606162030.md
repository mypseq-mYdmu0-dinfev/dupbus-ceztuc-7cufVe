38. Re 28 —— good news, and you're `~`95% right, with one caveat worth keeping for the "food safety" file:
- 38.1. Any TEMPERATURE programme (120°C, 65°C, up to your 190°C max) is genuinely safe —— the cooker regulates and physically can't exceed it. 190°C is comfortably under 260°C. Nothing to change there.
- 38.2. The ONE exception is the "100%" mode —— that's a POWER setting, not a temperature one, so the 190°C ceiling does NOT apply to it. You said it yourself earlier: at 100% the pan "never stops heating" and passes 200°C. On an EMPTY pan, full power can still climb past 260°C.
- 38.3. Why you're still fine in practice: with food or water IN the pan, the contents pin the temperature (boiling water 100°C, frying `~`180°C) no matter the power. The danger only exists if 100% runs EMPTY —— the old dry-fire habit you've already dropped.
- 38.4. Cleanest total fix (optional): do your cooking on a 190°C TEMPERATURE programme instead of 100% power. Then it's physically impossible to exceed 190°C, ever —— food-safety question fully closed, and you lose nothing (eggs/noodles/prawns never need more). Keep 100% only if you like the faster ramp, and only ever with food in.

41. Re 35.1.2 / 35.2.2 / 35.2.4 —— your height instinct is correct, and here's the rule that proves it:
- 41.1. At a FIXED diameter, capacity ∝ height. Your Tefal is 18cm/1.9L and "just fits". So anything with MORE than 1.9L at 18cm is taller and won't fit —— the Anolon X and Circulon are both 18cm/2.8L, i.e. `~`47% more volume = clearly taller. Too tall, as you suspected.
- 41.2. The 20cm ones are out on diameter alone.
- 41.3. Do this once: measure your current Tefal's external height with a ruler. That number is your dishwasher ceiling —— then you can judge any pan instantly from its size/capacity without guessing.

48. The "lifetime warranty" myth —— worth saying plainly, because Le Creuset, Essteele and Scanpan all wave it about:
- 48.1. These warranties cover MANUFACTURING DEFECTS and the body (warping, handle, delamination faults) —— NOT the non-stick coating wearing out, which is treated as normal wear.
- 48.2. And as the Le Creuset reviewer found, "dishwasher safe" on the box doesn't stop them blaming the dishwasher when the coating dies. So for you, the lifetime warranty is mostly decorative —— it won't replace the one part that always fails.

49. Overall —— the field is now exhausted, and every road leads back to where you stood:
- 49.1. No buyable pan is integral AND non-stick AND fits your dishwasher AND escapes the coating clock. The premium ones add a tougher body and a badge, then fail at the coating just the same —— the Le Creuset review and Scanpan's own care notes prove it from outside my opinion.
- 49.2. So: keep the $30 Tefal as the `~`4-month consumable; your 190°C hob can't harm the coating (just don't run 100% empty); switch to a pump mister if/when you fancy slowing the wear. Nothing left to buy, and that's the genuinely optimised answer —— not a defeat.
