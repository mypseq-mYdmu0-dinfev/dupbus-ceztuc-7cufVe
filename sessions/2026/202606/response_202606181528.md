# Response to query_202606181528.md
*Interviewed folder —— AJAP collision check*

1. Verdict —— your instinct is correct: as drafted (`seek/gcl/interviewed/`, a child of `/gcl/`), it CAN confuse AJAP. But it is cleanly solvable, and there is a structurally safer placement that makes "never touched" near-automatic. The copy-not-move design itself is sound and does NOT weaken silent-skip (see pt 5).

2. The key structural fact —— EVERY AJAP folder scan is rooted at `/gcl/`. I grep-audited all governance files (`ajap.md`, `main_ajap.md`, `MA_hb.md`, `seek/CLAUDE.md`). There are two classes of scan:
- 2.1. **Explicit-list scans** —— hard-coded to `applied` / `pending` / `skipped`. A new sibling `interviewed/` is invisible to these. They are SAFE by default:
  - 2.1.1. K6 silent-skip match —— `ajap.md:166` (`/gcl/applied` + sub-folders, `/gcl/pending`, `/gcl/skipped` + sub-folders). `interviewed/` is a sibling, not a sub-folder of any → never matched.
  - 2.1.2. Session count N —— `main_ajap.md:25, :80` (`/gcl/{applied,pending,skipped}` only).
  - 2.1.3. Broad file check —— `main_ajap.md:157`, `MA_hb.md:55` (`applied|pending|skipped` only; also keyed on filename-TS, which on copies stays OLD).
- 2.2. **Whole-tree scans** —— say "all of `/gcl/`" or "anywhere in `/gcl/`". A child `interviewed/` IS swept by these. These are the collision points:
  - 2.2.1. Pre-Flight state —— `ajap.md:94` "contents in `/gcl/` (incl. all sub-folders)". ⚠️ PRIMARY risk: the Pre-Flight table has no row for `interviewed/`, so a copied AR matching the open job adds an unhandled second match → ambiguous routing.
  - 2.2.2. S0.1.2 fallback count —— `ajap.md:132` "files created in `/gcl/` (excl. `_archive`)". ⚠️ A copy's CREATION time = copy moment; bulk-copying near a session over-counts this fallback tally.
  - 2.2.3. Batch tripwire —— `MA_hb.md:30` "any file created since `latest_TS` anywhere in `/gcl/`". Low false-trip risk (single-job copies, normal names) but it still scans the folder.
  - 2.2.4. MA between-loop audit —— `main_ajap.md:11` "check ALL new files in `/gcl/`".
  - 2.2.5. Heartbeat Monitor file-watch —— `MA_hb.md:51`, `main_ajap.md:134` watch `/seek/gcl/`. ⚠️ Copying files into `/seek/gcl/interviewed/` mid-session fires spurious MA wakes.

3. Recommended fix (Option A) —— place it OUTSIDE `/gcl/`, e.g. `seek/interviewed/` (or `seek/gcl_interviewed/` to keep the mnemonic). Because all 2.2 scans are scoped to the literal path `/gcl/` (none use a `gcl*` glob), a sibling of `/gcl/` is structurally unreachable by every scan and every file-watch —— "never touched" becomes a property of the path, not a rule someone must remember. Only ONE governance line needed (pt 6). This is the cleanest guarantee.

4. Alternative (Option B) —— keep `seek/gcl/interviewed/` as you proposed, but then "never touched" depends on patching each whole-tree scan, and stays fragile to any FUTURE rule that says "scan all of `/gcl/`". Required edits:
- 4.1. `ajap.md:94` —— "(incl. all sub-folders EXCEPT `interviewed/`)".
- 4.2. `ajap.md:132` —— exclude `interviewed/` alongside `_archive`.
- 4.3. `MA_hb.md:30` —— tripwire scope "anywhere in `/gcl/` except `interviewed/`".
- 4.4. `main_ajap.md:11` —— audit "all new files in `/gcl/` except `interviewed/`".
- 4.5. Heartbeat watch (2.2.5) —— cannot be path-excluded easily; you would tolerate occasional spurious wakes (harmless, just noise).

5. What stays intact either way —— silent-skip is NOT weakened, because the ORIGINAL AR stays in `/gcl/applied/` with `Outcome: Applied`:
- 5.1. K6 catches re-encounters whilst the AR is `` `<`30 days `` old (filename-TS).
- 5.2. K4 (green `✔︎` on the SEEK card) catches them at ANY age, independent of the AR —— so even a `` `>`30-day `` interviewed job is never re-applied.
- 5.3. The `interviewed/` copy contributes NOTHING to matching (by design) —— which is exactly why it must be excluded, not half-scanned.

6. The mandate you asked for —— one explicit rule, added to `seek/CLAUDE.md § Absolute Rules` and echoed at the top of `ajap.md` + `main_ajap.md`. Note the existing "All other paths —— read only" already blocks WRITES; this adds the never-READ guarantee:

7. One naming note (optional, no action needed) —— the folder mixes two states ("interview scheduled" vs "interviewed/completed"). One folder is fine for your stated purpose; sub-split only if you later want to track outcomes separately.
