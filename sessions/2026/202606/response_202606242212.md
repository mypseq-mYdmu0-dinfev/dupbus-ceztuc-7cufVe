## Part 2 —— Why R2 Read Easier

7. Verdict-first. R2 leads with the conclusion/action ("Drop the tail", "Confirm alignment, change nothing"); R1 leads with explanation and buries the action mid-paragraph.

8. Flatter nesting. R1 adds sub-reasoning (2.3.1, 2.3.2) + meta-commentary; R2 mostly stays one level —— less to hold in working memory.

9. More, smaller sections with functional titles ("Our line on the RA", "Educate Lu", "Sampling reply") vs R1's few huge sections (one "Reconciliation" section = 4 dense paragraphs).

10. No rhetorical hedging. R2 cuts second-person framing ("the irony you should see", "you may resist but should weigh") that adds load without content.

11. Reply-ability (the actual numbered.md goal). R2's atomic points map to your `re 1.2.1` workflow; R1's fat bullets can't be replied to surgically —— "re 2.3" would target a paragraph of mixed claims.

12. Root cause: R1 honoured the #numbered SKELETON but not its SPIRIT —— it numbered paragraphs instead of atomising points. numbered.md currently mandates the skeleton but only weakly nudges the spirit ("bitesize short lines"), so R1-style slips through.
