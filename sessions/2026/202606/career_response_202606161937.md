1. Job read (Atlassian — Value Advisor, Value Management Office)
- 1.1. Role nature: highly influential individual contributor in the VMO; the office "exists to infuse all interactions... towards the fulfillment of the customers' strategic business needs with joint ownership of their long term success".
- 1.2. Core mandate: develop content/resources/assets to scale value management; shape & deliver strategic value engagements with customers; act as thought leader/trusted advisor to CxO execs (Atlassian-side & customer-side).
- 1.3. Pillars: customer relationship building (senior execs), financial acumen & advanced value articulation (financial modelling, business cases), critical thinking/solution innovation (own the value framework), executive storytelling, practice-building, driving innovation at scale, thought leadership, value-based selling enablement.
- 1.4. Must-have: 7⁺ yr in value/management/strategy consulting + quantitative research, exposure to cloud & digital transformation; mastery in financial modelling, business case development, value articulation at exec level; exceptional storytelling/stakeholder mgmt.
- 1.5. Net reading: their "value management" is enterprise value-based selling/customer value realisation —— quantifying and articulating the economic value (ROI) a SaaS solution delivers to the customer, to justify and expand the investment. Value is measured on the customer's side, in dollars gained.

2. My "value engineering" (per virtual presentation script, S 01:39–03:00)
- 2.1. Origin: forged in resource-constrained, highly competitive environments (Asia/Europe).
- 2.2. Method: audit resources the client already holds (venue, manpower), then engineer a solution that maximises value whilst minimising expenditure —— repurpose existing assets, consolidate production processes, optimise resource allocation across the project lifecycle.
- 2.3. Principle: never cut cost by compromising quality; extract maximal output/quality per unit of resource spent.
- 2.4. Net reading: this is the textbook discipline of Value Engineering (function ÷ cost optimisation, doing more with less). Value is measured on my/delivery side, as output-quality relative to resources consumed.

3. Are they different? — Yes, materially.
- 3.1. Locus differs: mine is supply-side/internal (how efficiently I deliver); theirs is demand-side/external (how much the customer gains from buying).
- 3.2. Aim differs: mine optimises cost-to-value in execution; theirs quantifies customer-side ROI to drive a purchase decision.
- 3.3. Yardstick differs: mine = quality/output per dollar spent; theirs = dollars of value created for the buyer.

4. Overlap or completely contrasting? — Partial overlap, not contrasting.
- 4.1. Shared ground: both centre on "value", both lean on business cases, financial framing, executive communication, and the instinct to maximise value rather than blindly cut.
- 4.2. Divergence: my craft stops at optimising delivery economics; theirs extends to modelling and selling the customer's realised benefit. We share the vocabulary and the optimisation mindset, but point them at opposite ends of the transaction.

5. Which covers more? — Atlassian's "value management".
- 5.1. As a practice it spans content, value frameworks, financial modelling, exec advisory, thought leadership, org-wide enablement and innovation —— broader in surface area than my project-level VE technique.
- 5.2. Worth noting: even in the formal discipline (SAVE International / BS EN 12973), "Value Management" is the strategic umbrella and "Value Engineering" is one technique applied at the design/delivery stage —— so VM out-scopes VE both in Atlassian's loose usage and in the formal sense.

- 6.1. It demands codified, hard-to-fake rigour: 7⁺ yr value/strategy consulting plus mastery of financial modelling and exec-level business cases —— years of structured practice, not easily shortcut.
- 6.2. My VE is rare and creative, but it is largely experiential/craft —— honed by doing, more attainable than the quantitative-consulting depth Atlassian wants.
