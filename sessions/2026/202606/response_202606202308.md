# Response to query_202606202308.md
*Observer-SA proposal: agree on the goal, recommend a hybrid; 15s vs 60s clarified; watchdog yes*

25. Re 22.1 (spawn an Observer SA) —— I agree with the GOAL (free MA's context for you), but with one tension that points to a refinement.
- 25.1. The value is real: a long debate shouldn't pin MA's context when you want to discuss other things alongside or after it. Worth solving.
- 25.2. The tension: the Observer role —— judging "objective achieved / can't get better" against THIS session's decision —— is exactly the role that most needs MA's native full context. Fully offloading it forces an expensive, LOSSY briefing of the whole session into an SA, and risks a weaker verdict than MA would give. So the Observer is, ironically, the worst single role to hand off wholesale.
- 25.3. My recommendation —— a hybrid that gets both: spawn a LIGHT Observer SA that does the MECHANICAL watching (track the board every 15s; flag repetition / circling / no-new-points; watch debater liveness) and PINGS MA when it suspects saturation or sees a `## User` block —— but does NOT write the verdict. MA stays free meanwhile and engages only on a ping; then it makes the final saturation/objective call (native context) and writes `THE_END` + the `## Observer` block + your `response_`. Cheap briefing (the SA only needs "flag when arguments stop advancing"), MA's context preserved, and the context-critical judgement stays with MA.
- 25.4. Your full-offload version is still viable if you prefer —— accept the heavy session-context briefing and a possibly-rougher verdict in exchange for MA barely touching the debate. And MA-as-observer (current) stays simplest for focused or already-large-context runs. I'd DEFAULT to the hybrid, but I'll build whichever you pick (and MA can choose the mode on the spot per session size / whether you want to keep working).
- 25.5. Caveat respected: in every mode, MA ALONE sets roles/stances (the only instance with full session context; a wrong call there wastes the whole run). Never delegated. MA also creates + declares the board (it is coupled to that roles/stances decision).

26. Re 22.2 —— agreed: 15s for the watcher, whether it is MA or an Observer SA.

27. Re 22.3 (15s vs 60s —— they are NOT the same thing):
- 27.1. 15s = the change-DETECTION cadence: a background loop checks the board's mtime every 15s and wakes the Observer ONLY when it changed. That IS the "woken by file change" mechanism —— there's no true instant fs-notify here, so we poll; 15s is just how fast a change is noticed.
- 27.2. 60s = an UNCONDITIONAL heartbeat: it wakes the Observer every 60s even if nothing changed —— the safety net that (a) catches a dead/stuck watcher and (b) lets the Observer check debater liveness.
- 27.3. So it is ONE background loop with two triggers: "changed" (≤15s) and "heartbeat" (60s). Not redundant —— the 15s never fires without a change; the 60s fires regardless.

28. Re your inspired watchdog question (separate) —— yes, you're right:
- 28.1. An Observer SA can itself stall/idle (we watched debater SAs come to rest before the foreground-watch fix —— any SA can). So MA needs a LIGHT watchdog over it: wake every `~`300s, confirm the Observer SA is alive and progressing (a heartbeat marker / recent board activity); if dead, re-spawn it. This is precisely AJAP's SA2-watchdog tier —— the right call.
- 28.2. The 300s watchdog is cheap (it checks a liveness marker, not the whole board), so MA's context stays mostly free —— the watchdog doesn't undo the Observer-SA benefit.
- 28.3. With the foreground-watch sustain loop the Observer SA should be robust for a normal-length debate; the watchdog is the prudent backstop, not the primary mechanism.
