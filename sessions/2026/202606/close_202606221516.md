## 2. Decisions (each forced by a test)
- 2.1. SAs sustain via a FOREGROUND watch-wait —— never `run_in_background` (that makes an SA come to rest unrecoverably). [tests 2–3]
- 2.2. `THE_END` (standalone line) is the sole stop signal; debaters never self-terminate or self-declare saturation. [test 3]
- 2.3. User intervention = a `## User` board block (highest authority); `STOP` → graceful close. Copy-paste terminal template at the file's bottom; MA/SAs ignore that section. [tests 3–4]
- 2.4. Point labels `[Letter][3-digit]`, bulleted + 2-space-indented, continuous (A001…); header `[HHmm]` at the real append moment. [test feedback]
- 2.5. DELTA reads (read only new lines via `tail`) —— the cost lever; safe because the board is append-only. [post-test-4]
- 2.6. Token gate on BOARD tokens, scaled to debaters (×4k; ≥50% bias-close, ≥100% close); whole-debate `~`200k as a closing cost + compaction check (incl. the Observer). [test 4–5]
- 2.7. Director/Manager model: in Hybrid, a light Observer SA digests + self-closes, BUT MA keeps OVERSIGHT and may also close —— MA is never asleep. [§55 correction; vindicated test 6]
- 2.8. MA close-sequence: after `THE_END`, WAIT for debaters to stop + RE-READ the final board, THEN verdict (the verdict is always board-grounded, never digest-grounded). [test 6 —— fixed a stale-read verdict]
- 2.9. Header-safe close (`^THE_END$`); keep `THE_END`/`STOP`/`FINAL REPORT` out of headers. [recurring watcher false-positives]
- 2.10. DEFAULT MODE (MA-as-observer) = the default (simplest/cheapest/reliable; no digest to degrade); HYBRID MODE (+ Observer SA) = for concurrent work / `#sprint`. [final, this close]
- 2.11. Idle-not-saturated → MA prefers a cheap NUDGE (board append "continue"/"consider X") over spawning a debater; add a debater only for a genuinely new stance.
- 2.12. Post-`THE_END` race: a debater's last pre-append step is a FULL `grep '^THE_END$'`, not just its delta.
- 2.13. Real (non-`#sprint`) debates SUGGEST `debate.md` improvements at the `response_` end —— ongoing refinement from practice, not just tests.
