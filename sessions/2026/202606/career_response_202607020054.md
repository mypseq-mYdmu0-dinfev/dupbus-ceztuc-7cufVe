# Response to career_query_202607020054.md
*Straight read (CIIW): keep the instinct, do NOT pitch "replace the stack"*

1. Verdict up front.
- 1.1. Good kernel: AI can automate a lot of this, and thinking in systems is exactly your edge.
- 1.2. Risky leap: "replace HubSpot / Rentman / Teamwork with GitHub + CC + a custom UI" would most likely backfire in the room.
- 1.3. The keeper is your own last idea: **augment, not replace** (pt 5). That version can genuinely impress.

2. re HubSpot — do NOT propose replacing it.
- 2.1. It is not a passive "web 2.0" tool; it runs workflows, sequences, forms, dedup, reporting, and email-at-scale with deliverability and sender-reputation infrastructure a git repo cannot replicate.
- 2.2. Your users are non-technical (Seb, admin, PMs). GH/CC is a developer workflow; building "HTML UIs for struggling mates" turns you into an unpaid software vendor and a single point of failure.
- 2.3. It dies when you leave (and you plan to, ~1 year). They explicitly want tools that "outlast you"; HubSpot is hireable-for, your bespoke system is not.
- 2.4. It contradicts your own submitted plan, where setting up HubSpot IS the first-90-day priority. Reversing that now muddles your message.
- 2.5. Elena is a former HubSpot coach. Proposing to rip out her core tool is not "challenge me", it reads as telling the expert her craft is obsolete. High blow-up risk.

3. re Rentman — even further out of bounds.
- 3.1. It is specialised AV rental/inventory software (asset tracking, availability, crew, quote-to-invoice); a git repo would reinvent a mature vertical product, badly.
- 3.2. It is the ops team's tool, not marketing's. Proposing to replace it on day zero over-steps your lane, which itself flags as naive.

4. re EOS — private analogy only.
- 4.1. Your scrum/agile read catches the cadence similarity, fair enough.
- 4.2. But EOS is company-wide management (Rocks, Level 10s, scorecards, accountability), not team-level software delivery like Scrum. Different scope.
- 4.3. Never voice "EOS is just scrum rebranded" to Elena; she is an EOS believer and it would land as dismissive.

5. re Teamwork / MCP — THIS is the winner; reframe the whole thing as augment.
- 5.1. Keep their tools; wire Claude/CC to work WITH them via their APIs or MCP connectors, so the AI does the grunt work whilst legacy users stay on the web UI at their comfort.
- 5.2. This respects her investment, is realistic at their size, and still showcases your AI-systems depth.
- 5.3. Tweak: do not advise switching platforms just to get MCP; if a tool lacks an MCP server, Claude can hit its API directly. Switching stacks is the same overreach in disguise.

6. On "challenge me" — calibrate.
- 6.1. The challenge she wants is sharp thinking about the plan and the marketing, not torching her toolstack.
- 6.2. Contrarian-for-its-own-sake reads as arrogance; reasoned pushback on assumptions reads as the drive she asked for.

7. Interview recommendation.
- 7.1. Do NOT open with "let's not use HubSpot". Hold the replace-the-stack idea entirely; it is a post-hire, trust-earned conversation at best, and dubious even then for a 5-person firm.
- 7.2. If AI-tooling comes up (your Q3/Q4 invite it), pitch the augment angle: "I would connect what you already use to Claude so the busywork runs itself."
- 7.3. Check the premise first. You are assuming the firm is "Claude-native"; more likely they use Claude casually, not CC/agentic. Your Q3 exists to find that out, so ask before you build any pitch on it.
- 7.4. Mind the lane. You are the marketing hire; re-platforming the company's ops is not your remit, and proposing it signals you have misread the job.

8. Net: brilliant systems instinct, keep it. The value is in making their existing stack sing with AI, not replacing it. The augment version can impress her; the replace version can sink you.
