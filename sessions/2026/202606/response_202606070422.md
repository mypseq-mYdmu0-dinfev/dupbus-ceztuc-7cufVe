*It's "word wrapping"; PDF script handles iWork + Office, not just .pages*

25. Re 16 —— what that approach is called
- 25.1. It's **word wrapping** —— breaking lines only at word boundaries (whitespace), never inside a word. Doing it manually to a fixed column in the source is **hard wrapping** (real newlines), as opposed to **soft wrap** (the editor only *displays* it wrapped, no newline inserted). I keep the comments to ~79 columns —— the long-standing convention (Python's PEP 8 caps lines at 79).
- 25.2. The thing I deliberately AVOID —— splitting a word across a line with a hyphen (`y-` / `our`) —— is **hyphenation** (a.k.a. character / break-anywhere wrapping). That's what justified text in e-readers and browsers does. So in short: word-wrap (kept) vs hyphenation (avoided).

26. Re 20.2 —— it converts much more than `.pages`
- 26.1. Supported formats → PDF: **iWork** — `.pages`, `.numbers`, `.key` (via Pages / Numbers / Keynote); **MS Office** — `.docx`/`.doc`, `.xlsx`/`.xls`, `.pptx`/`.ppt` (via Word / Excel / PowerPoint). Output goes into a `YYYYMMDDHHmm` subfolder, then the resulting PDFs are Finder green-tagged.
- 26.2. So "leave as is" is the right call: it drives SIX GUI apps. A Python port can't cleanly replace it —— LibreOffice headless (`soffice --convert-to pdf`) covers the Office formats but does NOT open iWork files, so you'd lose `.pages`/`.numbers`/`.key`. Keeping it as AppleScript is correct; it earns its place as a standalone tool.
