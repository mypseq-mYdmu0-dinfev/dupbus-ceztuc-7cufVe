## Debater B · turn 1 · 0625
- B: SUPPORT material improvement needed; OPPOSE A complacency

- B001. The caption opens soft and loses the scroll. "Bought an older investment property, or renovated one recently?" is a flat, closed yes/no that a feed-skimmer skips. On a screening filter the hook is where you out-write 200 other applicants, so this is material, not nice-to-have. Fix: lead with the loss, e.g. "Own an older or renovated investment property? You could be leaving thousands in deductions on the table —— without realising it." Concrete, curiosity-driving, still compliant (no guaranteed figure, just "could be").

- B002. The brief is NOT as concise as the spec demands and burns words on hedging. "The aim is a steady volume of qualified enquiries within the campaign window at an efficient cost per lead" restates the objective sentence almost verbatim —— pure padding inside a 150`~`200w cap. Material: "practical and concise" is an explicit assessment criterion. Fix: delete that second sentence outright and reinvest the words in a target number (see B003).

- B003. The business-outcome link is asserted, not quantified —— a real risk against "connect marketing activity to business outcomes". "tying campaign spend directly to schedules sold" names the chain but never closes it with a figure. A hiring manager wants to see you think in a funnel. Material. Fix: add one illustrative target, e.g. "Target: ~40 qualified enquiries at a sub-$80 CPQL over the EOFY window, at the historic enquiry-to-schedule rate that returns positive ROAS." Even illustrative numbers signal commercial literacy; their total absence reads junior.

- B004. Two CTAs, two different verbs, zero consistency —— a small but visible polish miss. Brief says "See what your property could claim, request your free, no-obligation estimate"; caption says "See what your property could hold." "Claim" vs "hold" across the same submission looks like an unproofed draft. Nice-to-have leaning material on a filter that rewards rigour. Fix: pick one and mirror it in both —— "hold" is the safer compliance verb, so standardise on "See what your property could hold."

- B005. "Reddit investor communities mined for audience insight" is a liability dressed as initiative. It is the one channel line that invites a follow-up question (privacy, brand-safety, scraping optics) and adds nothing to the outcome chain. Nice-to-have to cut, but cheap insurance. Fix: replace with a defensible insight source —— "with PropertyChat and investor forums monitored for objection and language insight" —— same intent, no scraping connotation.

- B006. The caption never says who Duo Tax is or why trust them —— it sells the category, not the client. "A qualified Quantity Surveyor can assess what your property is eligible to claim" is true but brand-agnostic; any competitor could run it. Material on a brief whose whole point is generating enquiries FOR Duo Tax. Fix: one trust anchor before the CTA, e.g. "Duo Tax has prepared thousands of ATO-compliant schedules for Australian investors —— see what yours could hold." Differentiates without a guarantee.

## Debater C · turn 1 · 0626
- C: SUPPORT verify-and-correct accuracy/compliance

- C001. The core premise checks out and is well-judged. Older or renovated residential genuinely retains Division 43 capital-works potential; the 9 May 2017 change only clipped second-hand plant and equipment (Div 40) in established property bought after that date, leaving the building structure untouched. So the deliverable is correcting a real misconception, not manufacturing one. Accurate.
- C002. "Even those completed by a previous owner" is correct and is the sharpest factual point in the copy. Renovations after 15 Sep 1987 remain claimable under Div 43 regardless of who did them, and a registered-tax-agent QS may estimate historical costs where records are missing. Reads as genuinely expert to a QS firm. Keep it.
- C003. One silent qualifier worth naming: "the building itself ... may still hold genuine depreciation potential" is only true where construction commenced after 15 Sep 1987. For a pre-1987 structure the original build yields no Div 43. The hedge "may" carries it and I would not fail it, but if a single safe clause can be spared, "depending on construction date" or "subject to eligibility" closes the only real accuracy gap.
- C004. Compliance is sound on the regulated points. No dollar figure, no "you will save", no personal tax directive; "may", "could", "eligible", and "free, no-obligation estimate" are all the safe register. The caption defers to the QS to assess eligibility rather than advising the reader. No guaranteed-savings breach, no personal tax advice. Compliant.
- C005. The one phrase that drifts is "one of the simplest ways to strengthen your investment's cash flow" —— stated flatly as fact. Depreciation is a non-cash deduction that *can* improve after-tax cash flow, so softening to "can help strengthen" keeps it truthful and on the right side of the no-guaranteed-benefit line. Minor, but it is the only outcome assertion not hedged.
- C006. "ATO-compliant schedule that lasts for years" is accurate and, if anything, understated —— a schedule runs the property's 40-plus-year claim life. No overclaim. The QS fee being tax-deductible is a true, usable hook the copy has not yet spent.
