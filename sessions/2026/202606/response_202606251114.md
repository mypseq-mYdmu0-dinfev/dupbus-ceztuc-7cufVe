# Response to "No not yet the msg to them yet. There should be automatic means to waive the annual fee. Very common in HK, like HSBC, HASE, etc. all have it"
*Self-service waiver —— what Citi HK actually offers*

---

25. You are right about HSBC/HASE —— they expose an in-app self-service "waive annual fee" request. But Citi HK is **not the same**, and (more importantly) it would not fix your case anyway. Two separate points.

26. What Citi HK actually documents (CIC-validated live):
- 26.1. The stated waiver route is the **CitiPhone hotline 2860 0333** ("call to apply for annual fee waiver") —— no public in-app self-service waiver button like HSBC/HASE.
- 26.2. The app's self-service is **Pay with Points** (redeem ThankYou points to offset charges), NOT a fee-waiver request. A years-dormant card likely has no/expired points, so this is a dead end here.
- 26.3. The other "automatic" waiver = meeting an annual **spend** threshold (e.g. HK$100k) —— irrelevant, you did not spend.

27. The decisive nuance —— even if Citi had HSBC's button:
- 27.1. Self-service/automatic waivers **pre-empt the NEXT fee**; they do NOT reverse a fee **already charged** and accruing interest.
- 27.2. Yours is already posted (24 May) and has grown HK$1,800 → HK$2,148.92. Reversing a posted fee **plus** its interest needs a human (chat or phone) —— no self-service path reverses that.

28. So the automatic route is a non-option for your situation, not because I would not use it, but because it structurally cannot undo an already-billed, interest-bearing fee.

29. Before you accept that, a 30-second check on your side (in case Citi added something new):
- 29.1. In the app, look under the card's **Services / "Manage card"** menu for any "annual fee" or "Pay with Points to offset" option.
- 29.2. If one exists and offers to reverse the posted fee → use it and skip the message.
- 29.3. If it only offers to waive a FUTURE fee, or nothing → the agent message (pt 22, last response) is the route.

30. My read: 29.3 is almost certain, so the chat/phone request remains the real fix. Want me to (a) dig further to validate whether the CitiPhone IVR has a self-service waiver when you call, or (b) hold the message ready for when you have checked the app? Tell me which.
