## 2. Decisions (each forced by a test)
- 2.1. SAs sustain via a FOREGROUND watch-wait —— never `run_in_background` (that makes an SA come to rest unrecoverably). [tests 2–3]
- 2.2. `THE_END` (standalone line) is the sole stop signal; debaters never self-terminate or self-declare saturation. [test 3]
- 2.3. User intervention = a `## User` board block (highest authority); `STOP` → graceful close. Copy-paste terminal template at the file's bottom; MA/SAs ignore that section. [tests 3–4]
- 2.4. Point labels `[Letter][3-digit]`, bulleted + 2-space-indented, continuous (A001…); header `[HHmm]` computed at the real append moment. [test feedback]
- 2.5. DELTA reads (read only new lines via `tail`) —— the cost lever; safe because the board is append-only. [post-test-4]
- 2.6. Token gate on BOARD tokens, scaled to debaters (×4k; ≥50% bias-close, ≥100% close); whole-debate `~`200k as a closing cost + compaction check (incl. the Observer). [test 4–5 token analysis]
- 2.7. Director/Manager model: a light Observer SA digests + self-closes, BUT MA keeps OVERSIGHT (watches the digest) and may also close —— MA is not asleep. [§55 correction; vindicated test 6]
- 2.8. MA close-sequence: after `THE_END`, WAIT for debaters to stop + RE-READ the final board, THEN write the verdict. [test 6 —— fixed a stale-read verdict]
- 2.9. Header-safe close detection (`^THE_END$`); keep `THE_END`/`STOP`/`FINAL REPORT` out of headers. [recurring watcher false-positives]
- 2.10. Recommendation (not yet applied): DEFAULT to MA-as-observer; reserve the Observer SA for heavy concurrent-work runs.
