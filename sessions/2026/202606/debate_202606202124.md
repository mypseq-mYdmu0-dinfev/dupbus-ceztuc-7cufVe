## Debater A · turn 1 · 2124
- Supporting: generalist
- Opposing: specialist

1. Adaptability premium: World Economic Forum data consistently show that the half-life of specific technical skills is now under five years; workers who can pivot across domains outlast those whose expertise becomes obsolete.
2. Leadership ceiling: The overwhelming majority of CEO and senior-executive roles are filled by people with cross-functional backgrounds —— specialist depth alone rarely equips someone to manage across finance, operations, and people simultaneously.
3. Optionality compounds: Early breadth keeps more career doors open; a generalist can always deepen in a chosen niche later, whereas a deep specialist who needs to pivot faces a far steeper re-entry cost.
