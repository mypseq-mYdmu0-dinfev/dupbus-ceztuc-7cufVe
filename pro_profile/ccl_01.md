# CCL (Consulting Cover Letter)

When the job post is for a consulting role (MBB, Big 4, Simon Kucher, or similar), fetch and follow gcl.md BUT only use ACIC (not `web_search`, immediately stop if failed) for research AND apply additional requirements in below prompts to `6. ** Cover Letter:**` section with more stringent research (ensuring 100% accuracy), superior writing quality, and professional prudence.

## Firm Research Requirements
Before writing, research:
- Firm's specific values and culture statements
- Recent publications, thought leadership, case studies
- Office-specific focus areas or specialisations
- Methodologies or frameworks the firm promotes

## Structure Requirements
- Maximum 1 page, 3-4 concise paragraphs
- STAR Method: Structure examples using Situation, Task, Action, Result
- Quantified Impact: Include specific metrics and measurable outcomes in every example
- Structured Thinking: Demonstrate exceptionally logical problem-solving approach

## Content Priorities
1. Opening: Brief introduction connecting my bg to consulting (specifically their consulting e.g. strategy/operational, if applicable) and justifying motives to apply (e.g. for entry-level/grad programmes: frame as intentional repositioning into AU consulting market, not career regression)
2. Body Paragraphs (2-3): Each focused on core consulting competencies:
   - Problem-Solving & Analytical Rigour: Use data analytics, strategic thinking examples
   - Leadership & Impact: Show cross-functional leadership, team management
   - Client Service & Stakeholder Management: Demonstrate relationship building, external/official communications
3. Firm-Specific Paragraph: Research and reference specific firm values, recent publications, methodologies, or projects, leveraging on or connecting to above paragraphs
4. Closing: Confident, forward-looking conclusion

## Language & Tone
- Consulting Terminology: Use business analysis, value creation, operational excellence, strategic transformation
- Structured Language: "I identified...", "I analysed...", "I implemented...", "resulting in..."
- Firm-Specific: Never use generic content that could apply to any consulting firm
- Professional Confidence: Avoid hedging language; be direct and results-focused

## Key Differentiators
- Business Impact: Focus on revenue growth, cost reduction, efficiency gains, meaningful changes
- Methodology: Reference frameworks, analytical approaches, systematic problem-solving
- Adaptability: Show experience across industries/functions
- Innovation: Highlight creative solutions and transformation initiatives

## Mistakes to Avoid
- Generic statements applicable to any consulting firm
- Resume repetition without added narrative
- Verbose explanations; prioritise clarity and impact
- Weak quantification ("increased efficiency" vs "reduced processing time by 35%")
- Academic/theoretical focus without practical, real-world business outcomes
