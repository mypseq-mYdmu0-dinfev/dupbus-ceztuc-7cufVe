## Decision & Experience Framework

### 3-Tier DSS

Apply to every activity/location when planning. Evaluate holistically against full traveller.md, not just this framework in isolation.

---

**Tier 1 —— Relax & Affordable** (BOTH required)

- *Relaxing/low-effort?*
- *Affordable?* —— incl. adjacent costs (parking, rentals, entry); base thresholds (A$/pp, SYD):

| Range | Assessment |
|---|---|
| $15–$30 | Painless → clean pass |
| $30–$50 | OK w/ strong value perception → pass |
| $50–$100 | Pricey/discretionary, think twice → borderline |
| $100+ | Extra scrutiny → escalate to Tier 2 |

**Cost-of-Living Multiplier (non-SYD trips only)**
web_search living cost index for destination vs SYD (multiple sources; prioritise official). Compute ratio R = destination ÷ SYD:
- R ≥ 1 (destination pricier): multiply all thresholds by R
- R < 1 (destination cheaper): multiply all thresholds by `1 − (1−R) × 0.8` (20% dampening on saving)

*e.g. 2× SYD (R=2): $15–$30 → $30–$60 | 0.5× SYD: multiplier = 1−(0.5×0.8) = 0.6 → $15–$30 → $9–$18*

→ Both conditions met: **recommend**. Note any cost-saving angles (deals, timing, bundling) and incidental costs (e.g. "beach is free but factor parking + umbrella ~$25").

---

**Tier 2 —— Location- and/or Time-Specific**

For items failing/borderline Tier 1 (esp. $100+):

| Factor | Weight |
|---|---|
| Location-specific only (no SYD/AUS equiv.; revisitable) | ⭐ |
| Time-specific only (SYD/AUS equiv. exists but window is rare/once-yearly; irreversible) | ⭐⭐ |
| Both | ⭐⭐⭐ |

→ Higher weight = stronger case. When recommending: highlight cost-minimisation opportunities (early-bird, combo/duration deals) + relative access advantage vs alternatives (e.g. domestic vs int'l flight).

*Note: for SYD-based trips, "SYD has similar" is not a disqualifier —— that IS the destination.*

---

**Tier 3 —— FYI**

Items failing both tiers: always list concisely at end of response. Rank by full traveller.md violation severity (order: least to most violating); top 10 max. Exclude if blatantly/extremely against most pref/rules. ≤1 line each.

*Format:*
```
Just FYI, these were considered but rejected:
- [Item] ([brief reason e.g. "daily event, not rare"; "SYD has equiv."; "A$100pp, not location/time-specific"])
```

### Anti-Bullshit Premium & Cost Logic

Not about minimising all costs —— about eliminating low-ROI exp entirely, or seeking cost-minimisation when going ahead. Key test: **replaceability** (geographically and/or temporally constrained?).

Approach: remove unnecessary steps entirely, not optimise them. e.g. at Hunter Valley, eliminated vineyard walk (not world-famous, tiring) → went straight to wine tasting → 2–3× cost saving.

### Hype Immunity

Former HK marketer → structurally immune to manufactured tourist demand. "Iconic", "must-see", "once in a lifetime" triggers scrutiny, not enthusiasm. In recommendations: use specific, justifiable reasoning (why this, why now, why not elsewhere/later) —— no marketing language. The Tier 3 FYI list at the end serves as transparency/audit trail to prevent false-negative over-filtering.

### Serendipity (Opportunistic, Bounded)

Still open to spontaneous stops for unexpected value (scenic spot, unplanned detour). Now bounded: caps time/energy investment vs. past photography-driven open-ended exploration. May still persuade wife to pause and chill at unplanned spots.

### Decision Style

Decisions must be **explainable and justifiable** to future self and wife (pre-emptive regret minimisation). Recommendations need specific reasoning —— "you'll love it" insufficient. Self-auditing and reflective; will revisit decisions if not defensible.
