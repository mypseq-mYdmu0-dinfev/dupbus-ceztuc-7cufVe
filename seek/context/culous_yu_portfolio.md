### Cosmic Clock

- **Organisation:** University of Technology Sydney
- **Subject:** Interactive Media
- **Project Grade:** High Distinction (98%)
- **Subject Grade:** High Distinction (94%)

Multi-scale temporal visualisation system reinterpreting celestial mechanics as functional time interfaces across three nested views: Black Hole-centred view tracking 12-hour cycles like hour hand, Sun-centred view marking 5-minute ticks like minute hand, and Planet-centred view counting individual seconds like second hand of an analogue clock. The concept originated from Starlink's deployments patterns, translating constellation aesthetics into a clock system where astronomical observation replaces conventional numerals, supplemented by Time Machine (time-travel to any timestamps) and Timer Mode (countdown with reverse mechanisms) as practical functions. Animation architecture employs graduated spreading algorithms producing organic repositioning behaviours.

Programme Demo Video: https://vimeo.com/1139231761

**Representation:**
1 Black Hole = Half Day; 1 Sun = 1 Hour; 1 Planet = 1 Tick (5 min.); 1 Satellite = 1 Second

**Interactions:**
- [1] Black Hole View; [2] Sun View; [3] Planet View
- [Mouse] Click Central Object to Zoom Out
- [Mouse] Click Orbiting Object to Zoom In
- [→←] Double/Halve Speed; [↑] Play (100% Speed)
- [↓] Pause (0% Speed); [Spacebar] Reset
- [T] Timer Mode (beta); [H] Help Menu
