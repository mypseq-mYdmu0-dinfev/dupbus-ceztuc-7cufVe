## C. Architectural / Protocol Issues

### C1. MA-SA communication timing: 60s S6.4.2.5 wait is incompatible with async Agent notification model
- **Severity:** Critical (root cause of all SA idle incidents in this session)
- `ajap.md` S6.4.2.5: SA waits at S6.4.2.5 for MA to write "Submit then proceed to next card", up to 60s
- MA is invoked via task notification only when SA *terminates* — MA cannot respond within 60s of SA reaching S6.4.2.5 while SA is still running in background
- This created a recurring loop: SA reaches S6.4.2.5 → times out after 60s → goes idle → terminates → MA is notified → MA writes approval → spawns new SA → cycle repeats
- SalesHQ required 3 SA spawns to complete one submission; Zeekr required 2 SA spawns and was still unresolved at session end
- **Protocol fix needed:** `ajap.md` S6.4.2.5 / `main_ajap.md § Between-Loop Audit` should be updated to account for the async model (e.g. SA reads a pre-written standing approval rather than waiting; or SA checks ma_msg.md at S6.4.2.5 with a much longer timeout; or MA writes approval proactively at a specific safe point before S6.4.2.5)

### C2. "Stop" message format written to ma_msg.md not defined in protocol
- **Severity:** Low
- When entering investigation mode, MA wrote: "Stop — AJAP suspended by user. Become idle immediately."
- No canonical "stop" format exists in `ajap.md`; SA is designed to treat non-Continue messages as instructions then wait for "Continue"
- If SA were still running, it would follow the instruction and wait — which is the desired result
- However, the format is ad-hoc; a canonical stop signal should be defined in `ajap.md`

## E. Additional Findings — Session Log & Chat History

### E1. Session log severely under-populated
- **Severity:** Medium
- `ma_202605251707.md` contains only 3 lines: start state and one incident entry
- `main_ajap.md § Between-Loop Audit` (session log rules): each SA retirement is a reportable incident
- At minimum 3 SA retirements occurred in this session (SA #1, SA #2, SA #3 all went idle and were re-spawned) — none logged individually
- Only the general "SA timing" note at N=820 was logged; no per-incident entries with timestamp and N per the required format

### E2. MA verified CL during Pre-Flight, not Between-Loop Audit
- **Severity:** Medium
- Chat history (line 17): "CL was already verified clean in the pre-flight read" — MA read and assessed the CL during Pre-Flight Check
- The intended flow: MA audits the CL only upon receiving `[AR_PATH] | Applying | [N] | [FLAGS]` (the canonical SA loop report at S6.4.2.5)
- Pre-Flight is for determining tab/AR state, not CL content review
- By checking CL during Pre-Flight, MA bypassed the formal Between-Loop Audit gate

### E3. MA never received canonical SA loop reports
- **Severity:** High (structural)
- All SA loop reports (`[AR_PATH] | [OUTCOME] | [N] | [FLAGS]`) are sent via SA's chat output
- Since SA runs as a background Agent, MA only receives SA's terminal summary — not intermediate chat messages
- The canonical `[AR_PATH] | Applying | [N] | [FLAGS]` report at S6.4.2.5 was never delivered to MA in the expected format
- MA instead inferred state from file system and tabs — a workaround, not the intended protocol
- This is the same architectural gap as C1 (60s timeout) but from the report-delivery angle

### E4. Session log incident format not followed
- **Severity:** Low
- Required format: `printf '[timestamp] N=[N] [incident]\n' >> file` using `TZ='Australia/Sydney' date +"%H:%M"` for timestamp
- Actual log entry: `17:30 N=820 SA timing: went idle twice at S6.4.2.5 (MA-SA timing lag); submission confirmed Applied after second spawn`
- The format is close but the Bash command used was `printf '%s\n' "$(TZ=...date) N=... incident"` — result is correct; minor

### E5. Multiple SA spawns not individually recorded in ma_state.md per event
- **Severity:** Low
- `ma_state.md` was overwritten with each new SA ID; no history of prior SAs is retained
- Not a protocol violation per se (the rule only requires recording current SA ID), but makes post-hoc reconstruction difficult
