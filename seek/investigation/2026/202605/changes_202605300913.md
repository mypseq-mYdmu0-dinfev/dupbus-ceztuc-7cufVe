## Root Cause Analysis

**RC1 (primary): `cc_reminder.md` reschedules heartbeat at 300s, not 60s.**
`main_ajap.md` schedules the initial heartbeat at 60s (per Change 02 of `changes_202605300615.md`). But when the heartbeat fires and MA re-reads `cc_reminder.md`, it follows that file's rescheduling instruction — which still says 300s. So all subsequent heartbeats are at 300s intervals. With SA's 300s polling window, there is effectively only one heartbeat per polling period, making the timing too fragile to reliably approve or correct within the window.

**RC2 (secondary): Heartbeat active SA check has no compromised-CL path.**
Step 2 only writes the submit approval when the CL is clean. If the CL contains a banned word (e.g. "transformative"), the heartbeat does nothing — sa_msg.md stays "Continue", SA polls until all 20 checks are exhausted (300s), then stops idle. MA only receives the report after timeout, too late to intervene.

**RC3 (secondary): Compromised-CL correction message omits form re-paste instruction.**
Both the Between-Loop Audit and the new heartbeat correction write: "Fix the CL in the AR, then re-report." SA edits the AR file, but the corrected text is never pushed back into the Tab 3 SEEK application form. On subsequent approval, SA submits the original compromised CL that is already pasted in the form. Fix: instruction must also direct SA to run F3 (close Tab 3, re-duplicate Tab 2) and proceed from S6 so the corrected CL is pasted fresh.

**Primary concern on Change 03**: step 2a checks `ma_msg.md` ≠ "Continue" as the trigger. After writing a compromised correction, ma_msg.md contains the correction text. SA will have followed the instruction (fixed AR, run F3, proceeded from S6, re-pasted CL), and will be back at S6.4.2.5 re-reporting. At that point ma_msg.md still contains the correction text (SA never writes to it). Step 2a fires, re-reads CL from AR (clean), writes approval — SA submits. Clean resolution within ~120s of first compromised detection.

**Edge case**: if SA is fixing the CL (mid-F3) when step 2a fires, Tab 3 may not be at the review page yet. The check "Tab 3 still at review/application page" would not match, so step 2a correctly skips. Next heartbeat (60s later) re-evaluates.
