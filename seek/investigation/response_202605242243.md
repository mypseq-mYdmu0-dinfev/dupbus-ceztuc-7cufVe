4. **Tab-closure trigger vs. section checks — these are two separate mechanisms:**
- 4.1. Section-based checks are periodic (SA finishes a section → reads `ma_msg.md` → acts or continues)
- 4.2. Tab-closure trigger is event-based (SA discovers Tab 2–4⁺ gone mid-section → immediately reads `ma_msg.md`)
- 4.3. Both should coexist — section checks cover normal flow; tab-closure covers MA intervention
- 4.4. Detection method for involuntary closure: SA attempts to use the tab and finds it gone, OR SA explicitly checks tab list at the start of each section check — your call on which is simpler to specify in `ajap.md`

5. **Reset mechanism — who resets `ma_msg.md` to "Continue" after SA acts on an instruction?**
- 5.1. Option A — SA resets it immediately after reading and acting on a non-"Continue" message (before continuing work), so stale messages don't re-trigger on the next section check
- 5.2. Option B — MA resets it explicitly as part of writing the next instruction (MA always writes the full new content, overwriting whatever is there)
- 5.3. Option A is simpler operationally (MA never needs to think about resetting), but Option B is safer (MA has full visibility of current state at all times). Lean towards Option B — MA writes the complete new message each time, SA never self-modifies `ma_msg.md`

6. **SA idle/retirement detection:**
- 6.1. SA waits 15s × 4 loops (60s total) then becomes idle — but how does MA know SA has retired and needs re-spawning?
- 6.2. Suggest: SA writes a single line to `ma_msg.md` before going idle: e.g. `SA_IDLE` or `SA_TIMEOUT — waiting expired after 4 loops at [section]`. MA can detect this on its next check and re-spawn accordingly
- 6.3. Alternatively, MA's ScheduleWakeup heartbeat (every 5 min) implicitly catches this — if SA hasn't reported in >5 min and `ma_msg.md` still shows a pending instruction (not "Continue"), MA knows SA timed out

## On 5.1 (`/tmp/` location)

8. `/tmp/` on macOS is `/private/tmp/` (symlinked — `/tmp` → `/private/tmp`). It is **not** cleared between Claude Code sessions within the same boot; it is only wiped on system restart.
- 8.1. Practical implication: within a single day of heavy AJAP use (multiple sessions before a reboot), tmp files would accumulate across sessions
- 8.2. The session-start `rm -f` already handles the specific files listed, so extending it to cover all `/tmp/ajap_*` and `/tmp/CulousYu_*` at session start resolves this cleanly — no pile-up concern regardless of reboot frequency
