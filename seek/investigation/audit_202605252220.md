## C. Architectural Issues

### C1. SA spawned as foreground (blocking) Agent call — root cause of SA "going rogue"
- **Severity:** Critical
- **This directly answers the user's question: "why SA went rogue (not returning w/ loop report, leading to MA idle for an hour)"**
- SA was spawned using `Agent()` without `run_in_background=true`
- A blocking Agent call prevents the MA from taking any action until the SA terminates
- The SA did NOT go rogue — it correctly ran continuous loops per ajap.md, checked ma_msg.md after each sub-section, sent loop reports after each card, and stopped at S6.4.2.5 for MA approval
- However, because the call was blocking: (a) MA received zero loop reports during the run, (b) MA could not reset ma_msg.md between cards, (c) MA could not monitor or intervene in real time
- From the MA's perspective: "idle for ~50 min with no visibility" — from the SA's perspective: "operating correctly per protocol for ~50 min"
- The SA ran for ~50 min, 427 tool uses, processing 9+ cards; it only became visible to MA when it returned at S6.4.2.5 timeout
- **Fix required:** SA must be spawned with `run_in_background=true`; MA must then poll SA status and respond to loop reports as they arrive

### C2. S6.4.2.5 60s wait inherently incompatible with blocking Agent model
- **Severity:** Critical
- `ajap.md` S6.4.2.5: SA waits up to 60s for MA to write "Submit then proceed to next card"
- With a blocking Agent call, MA cannot write anything while SA is running — MA only regains control after SA terminates
- Result: every application requires SA to time out at S6.4.2.5, return to MA, MA writes approval, spawns fresh SA, fresh SA navigates back — minimum 2 Agent-call cycles per application
- This was observed every single time in this session: NaturesEnergy, HouseFinder each required at least 2 fresh spawns to complete
- Root cause is same as C1; both C1 and C2 are symptoms of the foreground Agent call choice

### C3. No canonical stop signal defined in ajap.md
- **Severity:** Low
- "Stop — AJAP suspended by user. Become idle immediately." is an ad-hoc phrase; not defined in ajap.md
- SA treated it as a non-Continue instruction per MA-SA comm rules → followed it and became idle after 60s → correct behaviour but based on implicit interpretation
- A canonical stop token (e.g. "STOP") should be defined in ajap.md so SA handles it deterministically
