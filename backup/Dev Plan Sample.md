## 8. Core Argument (Thesis)
FOM has built a production-grade commercial data platform (Salesforce Customer 360/Data 360) but its commercial data strategy is structurally constrained by three gaps: a physical attendance data silo, an underdeveloped governance framework, and expanding ethical exposure in B2B data monetisation. Recommendations address each gap through the module's strategic, ethical, and commercial lenses.

## 9. Key Theories & Frameworks (from Module)
- 9.1. **DalleMule & Davenport (2017/2020)** —— offensive vs defensive data strategy; FOM is over-indexed on defensive (GDPR compliance) and under-indexed on offensive (proprietary fan intelligence)
- 9.2. **3-Step Data Management Framework (Week 5)** —— Step 1: define business objectives; Step 2: data process management; Step 3: data architecture —— backbone for recommendations
- 9.3. **Analytics Maturity / DELTA Plus (Davenport, 2018)** —— FOM is descriptive-mature but prescriptive-immature; progression framing for strategic recommendations
- 9.4. **Journey to Intelligence (Week 6)** —— FOM's Salesforce deployment is a "journey to cloud"; recommendation is to evolve it to a "journey to intelligence"
- 9.5. **Khatri & Brown (2010)** —— data governance domains; absence of named ownership = governance domain failure (use carefully as pre-2020; pair with post-2020 practitioner source)
- 9.6. **Algorithmic ethics / GDPR purpose limitation (Week 6 + Savva, 2017; Zwitter, 2014)** —— anchor ethical exposure argument
- 9.7. **People in data management teams (Week 5)** —— process / technology / strategic people; use to frame governance recommendation (who owns FOM's data?)
