# Cheat Sheet 2 — Anticipated Questions, Stage 2 (Elena, in person)
*Stage 2 has three parts: (1) Values Alignment, (2) the Assignment working-session, (3) Realistic Job Alignment & Role Preview. Below, each question carries a one-line steer. Likely opener: last time Elena opened with "what would you improve about our website?", so expect a similar warm-up.*

---

## 1. Values alignment (how you work)
- **"Tell me about a time you took hard feedback."** → Radical candour is their value; give a real example and what you changed.
- **"How do you handle work being sent back through the review gate?"** → Scored as "ego about review gates". Welcome it early; mention your pre-approved-template idea to keep it fast.
- **"Describe owning a mistake."** → Ownership value; concrete, you fixed it and the system after.
- **"What does radical candour mean to you?"** → Direct AND caring; you practise it with teams.
- **"Small team, lots of banter — how do you fit?"** → You connect easily; independent but collaborative.

## 2. The assignment (defend the rework)
- **"Walk me through your biggest change."** → Lead here: front-loaded case-study extraction into the quiet Jul–Aug window, because their own brief says the team is unreachable Sep–Feb.
- **"Why cut case studies from 20 to 10–12?"** → 20 needs ~20 interviews in peak season; 1–2/month is the sustainable, benchmarked rate. Bank the raw material early instead.
- **"How do you extract early whilst still learning?"** → Week 1 is immersion; interviews run Weeks 3–4 whilst the team is reachable; you bank material, write later.
- **"5 case studies in August — real?"** → Yes; raw material was gathered in July, so August is a catch-up, not the steady rate.
- **"What did you keep, and why?"** → 3-phase shape, no posts till October, case-study-as-sales-asset, EOS cadence. The bones were good.
- **"What's the riskiest assumption?"** → The Pre-Start readiness (logins, assets, calendars on Day 1) sits with Elena; and approval-gate turnaround. You planned around both.
- **"Where did the market stats come from?"** → Cvent ANZ Event Industry Report 2026 (via Spice News). A named source, not a guess.
- **"Tell me about the AI systems you'd build."** → The agentic case-study engine (interview transcript → first-draft), social repurposing, AI B-roll. Offer to show how you work with it.
- **"Tell me about the Women in AV idea."** *(landmine)* → In-kind AV contribution at their Integrate event, not cash; confirm the exact body first. Then move on.
- **"What's the Rentman integration?"** *(landmine)* → Rentman = their gear-rental/inventory system; plan links it to HubSpot so deals and gear availability sync. Scope first, then build, maybe with light vendor help.

## 3. Realistic job alignment & role preview (fit and the hard parts)
- **"What part of this role least appeals to you?"** → Be honest but light; the heavy physical capture, manageable with crew/intern help and the client often supplying a videographer.
- **"Comfortable with on-site, physical content capture?"** → Willing; production background helps; you noted last time the client often attaches a videographer.
- **"This is hands-on and junior versus your GM background — why?"** → Deliberate: local Australian experience plus builder work you genuinely enjoy.
- **"How long do you see yourself here?"** *(loyalty landmine)* → Commit to the build and the growth path; do not over-promise tenure, do not volunteer a short horizon.
- **"Does this role fit what you actually want?"** → Yes: the build, the autonomy, and the AI mandate fit you well.
- **"Salary expectations?"** → Defer to offer stage: "happy to settle that if we get to an offer."
- **"When could you start?"** → 1–2 weeks (pro-bono advisory handover).

## 4. Background and motivation
- **"Walk me through your background."** → Use the WIB framework verbally: Marketing (your life), Growth ($1M first-year, KE), Coordinator (50-party VTC record), Builder-not-maintainer.
- **"Why Alltech?"** → Niche market, smaller competition, easier growth; you were genuinely impressed by their branding.
- **"Why a coordinator after senior roles?"** → A deliberate stepping-stone for local experience and hands-on building.

## 5. AI-depth curveballs (you leaned into AI, so expect these)
- **"Show me an AI system you've actually built."** → Have one ready: SpeakMate (your iOS GenAI app), or the AI-built video reel. Concrete beats abstract.
- **"What should AI NOT touch here?"** → Authentic event proof stays human-shot; judgement, brand fit, factual accuracy, and client approval stay human.
- **"What if we don't have the subscriptions you assume?"** → You can start lean, bring your own tools, and build with what is available; the value is the system design, not the seat.
