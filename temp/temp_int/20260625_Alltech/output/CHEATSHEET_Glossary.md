# Cheat Sheet — Glossary (Alltech)
*Priority-ordered, not alphabetical. One line each, just enough to sound fluent. Skim before the call.*

1. **EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System)** — the framework Alltech runs the whole business on, from the book *Traction*; a set of habits for staying aligned and hitting goals.
- 1.1. **Rocks** — the 90-day priorities each person owns (their quarterly goals).
- 1.2. **Level 10 Meeting** — the standard weekly team meeting format in EOS.
- 1.3. **Scorecard** — a handful of weekly numbers each role is measured on.
- 1.4. **Role Blueprint / IDP** — your defined accountabilities and individual development plan.
- 1.5. **"EOS cadence"** — the rhythm of those weekly meetings, quarterly Rocks, and scorecards (the phrase you used).
2. **HubSpot** — a CRM plus sales-and-marketing automation platform; where you would build the sales pipeline, email templates, and automations. Your first-90-day priority.
3. **CRM** — customer relationship management; the database of contacts, deals, and pipeline stages (HubSpot is one).
4. **Rentman** — cloud software for AV/event firms to manage gear rental, inventory, quotes, scheduling, and crew. The plan links it to HubSpot.
5. **Teamwork** — the project-management and task tool Alltech already uses.
6. **Case study** — a short, structured story of a past job (Challenge, Solution, Outcome) used as a sales and marketing asset.
7. **Sales enablement** — the materials that help sales close: service booklets, pitch decks, capability statements, one-pagers.
8. **Content pillars / content calendar** — pillars are your few recurring content themes; the calendar is the schedule of what posts when.
9. **Organic (vs paid)** — unpaid reach (posts, SEO, word of mouth) versus paid ads; Alltech is organic-only for the first 12 months.
10. **GROW EPIC** — Alltech's 8 values: Growth-mindset, Radical candour, Ownership, World-class communication, Empathy, Psych-safe, Inspire, Coachable.
11. **Radical candour** — direct, honest feedback delivered with genuine care; one of their core values.
12. **Assignment selling** — their sales style: educate prospects with content and case studies rather than hard-selling.
13. **CMS / WordPress / Wix** — content management system, the platform a website is built and edited on (Wix is likely theirs).
14. **SEO / GA4** — SEO = getting found on Google; GA4 = Google Analytics, the website-traffic dashboard.
15. **Peak season** — roughly September to February, when events are busiest and the crew is hardest to pull for case-study interviews.
16. **Agentic workflow / AI agent** — an AI system that carries out multi-step tasks on its own, not just a single chatbot reply; your core angle.
17. **Sales pipeline** — the stages a potential deal moves through, from first contact to won, tracked in HubSpot.
18. **AVIXA / Women in AV / Integrate** — AVIXA = the global AV industry body; its Women's Council is the "Women in AV" group; Integrate = the main ANZ AV trade show (ICC Sydney).
19. **Cvent ANZ Event Industry Report** — the 2026 survey your market stats came from (65% / 81% / 31%).
20. **AV basics** — LED (the big video walls/screens); staging and rigging (the physical build); vision (cameras, switching, broadcast); bump-in / bump-out (setting up / tearing down an event).
