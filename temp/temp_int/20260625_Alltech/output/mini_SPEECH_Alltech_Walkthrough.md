# Mini Speech — Taking Elena Through the Assignment (short)
*Half-length version of `SPEECH_Alltech_Walkthrough.md`, for when Elena is moving fast or seems pressed for time. Share the PDF and hit the spine only. ~4 minutes. Pause whenever she wants in.*

## Opening
1. Thanks for the time, Elena.
2. Rather than just send it over, let me take you through the thinking quickly, and stop me wherever you would like to dig in.

## What I changed (pages 1 and 2)
1. Your draft was genuinely well-planned, so I kept the bones, the 3-phase structure, no posts until October, the EOS cadence.
2. My job was to keep the ambition and remove the few things that would break in a real quarter at 24 hours a week.
3. It comes down to 5 principles, and the big one is the first, sequence around peak season.
4. Your draft back-loads the case studies into September to December, exactly when the team is flat out, so I pulled the interviews forward into the quiet July and August window and banked the material early.
5. That single move de-risks the biggest deliverable.
6. The other 4, briefly, phase the build rather than run 6 pillars at once, make the hours honest, respect the review gate on both sides, and calibrate to a cooling market.

## The plan (pages 3 to 5)
1. Pages 3 to 5 keep your Immerse, Build, Execute structure.
2. The main changes are staggering the Month-1 audits, front-loading those case-study interviews, and being precise about the word independent, because owning a workstream is not the same as finishing it.

## The number I moved most (page 6)
1. Page 6 is the milestones.
2. The headline is the case studies.
3. Your draft aimed for 20 by December, I brought it to 10 to 12, because 20 means interviewing through peak season, and 1 to 2 a month is the sustainable rate.

## AI (page 7)
1. Page 7 is how I make a 24-hour week actually work.
2. I build systems to do the heavy lifting and keep the judgement for myself.
3. Case studies from interview transcripts, 1 event turned into a week of social, AI for atmosphere but the real footage of your work stays human-shot.
4. The judgement, the brand fit, and the accuracy stay with me and with your approval.

## My questions (page 8)
1. And my questions on page 8.
2. The most important is how AI is used across Alltech today, and whether there is appetite to move from chatbot-style use to bespoke, agentic workflows, because that tells me how far I can take everything I just described.
3. I would also want to know how the current data and platforms look, how fast approvals turn around, which subscriptions are in place and whether I would have access, what should stay off-limits to AI, and what you would want Alltech to be known for in 18 months.

## Close
1. That is the short version.
2. It is a working version 1, so I would genuinely welcome where you would push back.
