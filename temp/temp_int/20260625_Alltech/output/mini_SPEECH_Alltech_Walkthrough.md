# Mini Speech — Taking Elena Through the Assignment (short)
*Half-length version of `SPEECH_Alltech_Walkthrough.md`, for when Elena is moving fast or seems pressed for time. Share the PDF and hit the spine only. ~4 minutes. Pause whenever she wants in.*

## Opening
1. Thanks for the time, Elena.
2. Not sure if you've read it yet, but let me take you through the onboarding plan quickly, and please stop me wherever you would like to dig in.

## What I changed (pages 1 and 2)
1. The original draft was genuinely well-planned, so I kept the bones: the 3-phase structure, no posts until October, the EOS cadence, etc.
2. I focused on keeping the ambition and adjusting things that could break in a real quarter at 24 hours a week.
3. It comes down to 5 principles, and the big one is the first one: sequencing around the peak season.
4.  The original draft back-loads the case studies into September to December, quite likely exactly when the team is flat out, so I front-loaded the interviews into the quiet July and August window and banked the materials early.
5. That single move managed a big risk.
6. The other 4, briefly speaking, phase the build rather than run 6 pillars at once, make the hours honest, respect the review gate on both sides, and calibrate to a cooling market.

## The plan (pages 3 to 5)
1. P.3 to 5 keep your IBE (Immerse, Build, Execute structure).
2. The main changes are staggering the Month-1 audits, front-loading those case-study interviews, and being precise about the word independent, because owning a workstream is not the same as finishing it alone.

## The number I moved most (page 6)
1. Page 6 is the milestones.
2. The headline is the case studies.
3. Your draft aimed for 20 by December, I brought it to 10 to 12, because 20 means interviewing through the peak season, and 1 to 2 a month is the sustainable rate.

## AI (page 7)
1. Page 7 is how I make a 24-hour week actually work.
2. I build systems to do the heavy lifting and keep the judgement for myself.
3. On case studies from interview transcripts, 1 event turned into a week of social, AI for B-rolls but the real footage of the work remains real.
4. The judgement, the brand fit, and the accuracy stay with me and with your approval.

## My questions (page 8)
1. And finally my questions are on P.8.
2. The most important one is how AI is currently utilised across Alltech, and whether there is appetite to move from chatbot-style to bespoke & agentic workflows, because that tells me how far I can take everything I proposed.
3. I would also like to know how the current data and platforms look, how fast approvals turn around, which subscriptions are in place, whether I would have access, what should stay off-limits to AI, and what you would want Alltech to be known for in 18 months.

## Close
1. That is the short version.
2. It is a working version 1, so I would genuinely welcome where you would push back.
