# EPW Universal Files Summary

## 1. Course/ Executive Project Weeks 2026 | VLE.pdf

Module homepage explaining EPW structure: three online weeks scheduled throughout MBA programme where students work in small groups on industry guest speaker-led projects, developing management/leadership skills through real-world challenges. Essential 10-credit experiential module.

## 2. Topic/ Assessment Preparation | Executive Project Weeks 2026 | VLE.pdf

Pre-MIP guidance on preparing for assessments whilst undertaking EPWs. Advises collecting evidence (screenshots, logs), using reflection tools (Gibbs' cycle), noting useful theories/frameworks. Outlines both assessment formats: individual report (70%) and portfolio (30%).

## 3. Executive Project Weeks 2026/ MGTK746 Reading List | VLE.pdf

Recommended academic readings for MIP module covering team building (Carson, Dyer, Goncalves), reflective practice (Hargreaves), strategy (Johnson et al.), leadership (Northouse), people management (Thomas, West), and self-reflection/questioning (Porter, Wood Brooks & John).

---

## Key Understanding Confirmed

**What EPWs are:** Intensive 1-week experiential learning events where MBA students collaborate in small groups (5-7 people) to solve contemporary business challenges introduced by industry guest speakers. Each EPW follows structured timeline: Tuesday introduction, Wednesday-Friday facilitated meetings (2hrs daily), Saturday group work, Sunday presentation submission, Monday plenary session.

**What they're intended for:** Applying theoretical knowledge to practical management scenarios, developing collaborative skills across diverse cohorts/geographies/industries, gathering evidence for subsequent MIP assessment module where students critically reflect on tools/theories used, data synthesis abilities, and collaborative performance.

# MIP Module Files Summary

## 1. Course/ MGTK746 Management in Practice January 2026 | VLE.pdf

Module homepage stating MIP assesses EPW learning through 2000-word report (70%) and portfolio (30%), due week 9. Runs parallel to other modules. No lecturecasts/synchronous sessions. Formative feedback weeks 3 and 7. Learning outcomes: apply tools/theories, critical reflection, data synthesis, collaboration, self-reflection.

## 2. ULMS - Personal Development Portfolio.pdf

Marking rubric with grade bands (90-100% to 0-29%). Highest marks require comprehensive detailed record, extensive critical/creative reflection, substantial reading, excellent presentation, systematic structure. Key criteria: reflection quality, evidence breadth, reading depth, presentation standard, structural coherence.

## 3. Topic/ Module Schedule | MGTK746 Management in Practice January 2026 | VLE.pdf

Timeline: Week 1 self-introduction (30/1), Week 3 Formative 1 (16/2), Week 7 Formative 2 (16/3), Week 9 Final A1+A2 (30/3 23:59 UK time). Career catalyst and module survey also required.

## 4. Topic/ Unit 1 | MGTK746 Management in Practice January 2026 | VLE.pdf

Unit 1 (weeks 1-3): familiarise with assessment briefs, reflect on EPW experiences, produce Formative 1 (outline structures for both assessments), introduce self to cohort, review reading list, contact lecturer via office.

## 5. MGTK746 January 2026/ Activity 2/ Formative Submission 1 | VLE.pdf

Formative 1 instructions: submit outline structures for both assessments (A1 as Word, A2 as PowerPoint) by 16/2. Can use full sentences or bullet points. Non-graded but provides formative feedback for final submissions.

## 6. MGTK746/ Management in practice (KOL) | University of Liverpool.pdf

Reading list identical to EPW universal file: Carson et al. (shared leadership), Dyer (team building), Goncalves (team building), Hargreaves (reflective practice), Johnson et al. (strategy), Northouse (leadership), Porter (self-reflection), Thomas (people management), West (teamwork), Wood Brooks & John (questioning).

## 7. Topic/ Unit 2 | MGTK746 Management in Practice January 2026 | VLE.pdf

Unit 2 (weeks 4-7): develop draft final submissions, read assessment briefs, produce Formative 2, receive formative feedback supporting final graded assessment.

## 8. Topic/ Unit 3 | MGTK746 Management in Practice January 2026 | VLE.pdf

Unit 3 (weeks 8-9): review Formative 2 feedback, refine/submit two graded assessments, complete career catalyst reflection, complete end-of-module survey. Reminds students to provide constructive respectful feedback.

## 9. MGTK746 January 2026/ Activity 1/ Final Assessment 1/ Individual Report (70%) | VLE.pdf

A1 detailed brief: 2000 words maximum (strict penalties for excess), structure = intro (100w) + 3 critical reflections (500w each: tools/theories, data sources, collaboration) + PDP (200-400w SMART actions) + references (excluded from count). First person for self, third person for theory. Harvard referencing. Draw on A2 portfolio. No primary research.

## 10. MGTK746 January 2026/ Activity 2/ Final Assessment 2/ Portfolio of Evidence (30%) | VLE.pdf

A2 detailed brief: 10 slides + 1 cover maximum (strict penalties for excess), 1000 words slide notes total (~100w per slide). Evidence types: deliverable contributions, leadership roles, facilitator/peer feedback, critical incidents, synchronous/asynchronous sessions. Typically 3 slides per EPW, 4 items per slide. Descriptive notes contextualising items. No introduction/conclusion. Harvard if references used. Marker assumes EPW format knowledge.

---

## Key Understanding: Full Marks Requirements

**A1 (70%) - 90-100% band criteria:**
- Comprehensive detailed critical reflection across all three areas
- Extensive evidence of reading/theoretical engagement
- Sustained critical analysis (not descriptive)
- Systematic structure with excellent presentation
- Well-written with proper Harvard referencing
- SMART PDP actions genuinely derived from reflection
- Clear cross-references to A2 evidence items

**A2 (30%) - 90-100% band criteria:**
- Comprehensive detailed evidence record
- Carefully structured with extensive range of evidence types
- Demonstrates contribution across all EPWs
- Professional presentation with clear labelling
- Contextual slide notes (not analytical - save for A1)
- Evidence supports all A1 reflection areas

**Critical success factors:**
- Evidence quantity and breadth (leadership, collaboration, tools application, feedback)
- Theoretical engagement depth (Carson, Northouse, West, etc.)
- Reflection quality (critical vs descriptive)
- A1-A2 integration (portfolio evidences claims in report)
- Strict adherence to limits (2000w, 10 slides, 1000w notes)
- SMART PDP with specific completion dates
