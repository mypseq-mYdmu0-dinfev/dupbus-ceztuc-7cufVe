### AD01.4. Key Learnings & Standing Rules
- AD01.4.1. **Cause and effect — qual vs. quant:** qual cannot imply causation; no arrows in conceptual framework; no "why" RQs; words like "impact", "measure", "assess", "effect" belong to quant only; applies to all future RQ drafting, interview guide design, framework visualisation
- AD01.4.2. **Qualitative RQ rule:** must start with "what" or "how" — never "why"; 1 central RQ + 2–3 sub-RQs; sub-RQs derived from lit review
- AD01.4.3. **Evaluative framing preferred:** evaluative or explanatory research types over purely exploratory; default lens "how effective is X?" or "to what extent does Y achieve Z?"
- AD01.4.4. **Mixed methods: avoid** — module-level directive (W5.2); not a hard ideation rule but design out during convergence where viable
- AD01.4.5. **Qualitative as module default** (W1.3) — most MBA projects expected to be interpretivist/qualitative; quant remains valid but requires stronger methodology justification
- AD01.4.6. **Transcription constraint:** MS Teams / MS Office / MS Copilot only — Zoom or any third-party = breach of research integrity; plan interview workflow accordingly
- AD01.4.7. **Multi-org design confirmed (Wk3 seminar):** 1 participant per org = no org-level consent letter needed; must be explained clearly in methodology section
- AD01.4.8. **Industry-wide rationale:** TPS is supra-organisational; studying it in one firm conflates findings with that firm's idiosyncratic culture; cross-firm sampling = methodological necessity; must appear prominently in intro
- AD01.4.9. **Ethics — safe framing rule:** all interview questions must stay at professional observation level (not personal emotional disclosure); avoids LOREC and keeps project in standard ethics approval territory
- AD01.4.10. **Researcher positionality:** Culous is a TP; acknowledged via interpretivism's reflexivity convention; not a disqualifier; provides reflexivity material for methodology chapter
- AD01.4.11. **"Fuelling vs. competing" insight:** interviewing mgrs = competing with practitioners; interviewing TPPs = uncovering what mgrs cannot see; TPPs as proximal experiencers is the academic justification for peer-primary design
