## Assessments

### A1 —— Research Proposal (1500w, 10%)
**Template sections** (word counts suggestive):
- Introduction (300w): management challenge, aims/objectives/RQs, contribution
- Literature Review (600w): critical review, gaps, thematic map/conceptual framework
- Methodology (600w): ontology/epistemology, data collection, sampling, analysis, reliability/validity, feasibility/limitations, ethics
- Milestone Plan (excl. word count)
- References (excl. word count)

**Word count includes:** in-text citations, student-produced tables/graphs, executive summary if present
**Excludes:** ref list, appendices (if not excessive), imported tables, contents page

**LOs assessed:** LO1 (select + justify challenge), LO4 (analyse/synthesise for reasoned judgements)

### A2 —— Recorded Presentation (≤10 min, 20%)
Audience = senior management team / client (not academics). Still Harvard referenced.

Required flow: welcome (ID the client) → purpose/importance → methods credibility → key findings (visually compelling) → recommendations → call to action → reference list

Submit as both **.pptx** (auto-Turnitin) and **.mp4**

**LOs assessed:** LO1`~`LO5

### A3 —— Final Project (≤8000w, 70%)
Format: Times New Roman or Arial 11`~`12pt, 1.5 line spacing, Harvard

**Chapter structure** (word counts advisory except exec summary):
| Chapter | Content | Words |
|---|---|---|
| Exec Summary | Purpose, methods, key findings & recs | ≤350 (incl. in count) |
| Ch1: Introduction | Problem background, aims/objectives/RQs, chapter structure | 800 |
| Ch2: Literature Review | Critical review, gaps, conceptual framework | 2500 |
| Ch3: Methodology | Approach, data collection (incl. piloting), sampling, analysis, trustworthiness, ethics | 850 |
| Ch4: Findings/Results | Data presentation (may merge with Ch5 for qualitative) | 1250 |
| Ch5: Analysis/Discussion | Relate findings to lit + RQs; "golden thread" | 1250 |
| Ch6: Conclusions & Recs | Conclusions, SMART recs, limitations, future research, personal reflections | 1000 |

Appendices must include: participant info sheet, blank consent form, ethics approval confirmation, interview schedule/questionnaire, sample data/transcript, analysis/coding example

**LOs assessed:** LO1`~`LO5

## ⚠️ Ethics —— Critical Constraints (topic-selection impact)

### What requires ethical approval
Questionnaire, survey, focus group, observation, tests, secondary data with personal info

### Hard exclusions (cannot do these → affects topic choice)
- Any vulnerable participant: children (<18), those unable to consent, those unable to refuse (economic/social coercion)
- Deception of participants
- Sensitive topics: bereavement, criminal activity, embarrassing/personal subjects
- Studies causing discomfort/anxiety/stress
- Anything requiring LOREC review → **change topic** (takes too long)
- Research on people over whom researcher has authority (e.g. own direct reports)

### Process (Wks 7`~`11) —— indicative, iterative
1. **Wk7:** Proposal approved by supervisor + second reviewer (2 loops possible)
2. **Wk8:** If in-company/institutional consent needed → obtain separately (can delay; no extension allowed)
3. **Wk9:** Ethics application approved by supervisor
4. **Wk11:** Ethics application approved by second reviewer → written confirmation → **only then can data collection begin**

> ⚠️ **LOREC referral = topic change signal.** Avoid topics that could trigger LOREC (high-risk).

### Transcription constraint
**Only permitted:** MS Teams, MS Office, MS Copilot
Zoom or any other platform = breach of research integrity

### Data storage
Must comply with GDPR; anonymise all participant data; survey invites must use BCC
