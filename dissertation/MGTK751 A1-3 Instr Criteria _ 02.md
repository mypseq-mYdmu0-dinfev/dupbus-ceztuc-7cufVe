# Recorded Presentation Submission (Assessment 2) —— Maximum 10 Minutes

**Opens: Monday, 16 November 2026, 12:00 AM**
**Due: Monday, 23 November 2026, 11:59 PM**

## Submission Instructions

- **Submission type:** Individual Recorded Presentation
- **Presentation Length:** The maximum length for this assessment is 10 minutes. As per the Assessment length policy, penalties will be applied to assessments which exceed this length. Please use the following coversheet when you submit the assessment.
- **Submission date:** Day 7 of Week 36
- **Weight:** 20% of the overall mark

## Task

Your assignment is to produce a recorded presentation with slide-based content and audio narrative. You are required to imagine that you are presenting to the senior management team / client(s) for your research project to persuade them to accept your findings and adopt your recommendations.

Your audience are managers rather than your lecturer or other academics. However, your slides should still be referenced using the Harvard style of referencing with in-text citations and an end reference list.

Your presentation should be professionally presented, well-structured and logical (use models such as the MECE Framework and MINTO Pyramid from lecturecast week 2 to help you) and persuasive (such as using storytelling from lecturecast week 6). You should:

- Welcome your audience (use this to let the lecturer know who the client is)
- Summarise the purpose and importance of your research —— what is the problem that you have the solution to? Why is this problem worth fixing?
- Summarise your research methods to persuade your audience of your competence as a researcher and the credibility / appropriateness of the methods you have employed.
- Summarise your main findings focussing on what is most relevant, interesting or surprising for your audience and presenting your data in a way that is visually compelling.
- Explain and justify your recommendations based on your findings (if you have many recommendations then just focus on the most important ones).
- Request what you need —— what do you need from your audience today e.g. agreement to a particular decision, financial or other resources, approval to submit a business case (you can do this here or earlier in the presentation if you prefer)
- End reference list —— this should be displayed for long enough for you to say something like "thank you for your time today - these are my references".

**IMPORTANT:** Each student must submit a copy of the assignment as both a PowerPoint presentation (.pptx) and as an MP4 file. The former will automatically be submitted through Turnitin after it has been uploaded to the submission portal.
