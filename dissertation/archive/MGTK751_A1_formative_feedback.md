The title is clear, focused, and intellectually interesting, with a strong positioning of Tall Poppy Syndrome (TPS) as a management challenge. The aim is well articulated and aligned with the title, though slightly dense in wording. The objectives are appropriate and show progression from literature to application. Importantly, the research questions are distinct and not repetitive of the objectives, which works well as they are more focused and exploratory rather than duplicative.

The literature review is one of the stronger elements. It demonstrates good theoretical grounding, particularly through KBV, social comparison, and social exchange theory. However, at times the writing becomes overly conceptual and assertive without enough critical evaluation of limitations or alternative perspectives. The conceptual framing is coherent and aligns well with the research focus, though it remains somewhat abstract. You could strengthen it by making clearer how it directly informs the research questions and data analysis.

The methodology is well aligned and thoughtfully justified. The interpretivist stance, cross-firm qualitative design, and focus on peer perspectives are convincing. Sampling is justified conceptually, though the minimum of six participants feels slightly limited and could be better defended in terms of depth and saturation. The discussion of trustworthiness and reflexivity is strong and appropriate for this stage.

Overall, this is a strong and well-developed proposal. The main improvement lies in introducing more critical balance in the literature and slightly sharpening the conceptual-to-empirical linkage.
