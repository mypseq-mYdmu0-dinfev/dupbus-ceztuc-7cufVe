# Enis' Feedback for `A1.md` (v09)

Grade: 70% (A)

Hi Culous,

Thank you for your submission. 

The research problem is original and clearly positioned and Tall Poppy Syndrome is framed not only as an interpersonal issue, but as a possible knowledge management and team cohesion risk in knowledge-based firms. The aim, objectives, and research questions are mostly aligned, and the use of Knowledge-Based View, Social Comparison Theory, and Social Exchange Theory gives the proposal a stronger theoretical base than a standard descriptive workplace behaviour project.

The literature review is relatively strong because it does more than summarise sources as it builds a logic between peer comparison, marginalisation, knowledge withdrawal, and organisational risk. However, the writing is sometimes too dense and over-theorised, which can make the management problem feel less accessible. Some evidence, such as consultancy or survey-based claims, should also be used more cautiously alongside stronger academic sources.

The methodology fits the topic well, especially the interpretivist stance, semi-structured interviews, and attention to disconfirming evidence. However, the proposed sample is quite small, and relying mainly on peers may produce partial accounts of Tall Poppy dynamics. Overall, this is a good proposal with originality and theoretical depth, but could benefit further from tighter writing and a more robust sampling strategy.
