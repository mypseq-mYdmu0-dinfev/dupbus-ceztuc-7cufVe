### Context

This is academic literature research for a 1,500-word MBA research proposal (Assessment 1) and an 8,000-word dissertation (Assessment 3) at the University of Liverpool. The topic is: **Tall Poppy Syndrome (TPS) as a management challenge in knowledge-based firms (KBFs)** — law, accounting, consulting, IT, R&D, etc.

- **TPS:** social phenomenon where high-performing individuals face peer marginalisation in response to standout performance
- **Core management challenge:** TPS may cause high performers to withdraw from knowledge sharing → KM governance risk + team cohesion erosion
- **Research method:** qualitative, semi-structured interviews with peers of high performers (TPPs) across multiple KBF organisations
- **Key framing rules:**
  - KM withdrawal = a hypothesis to investigate, NOT an assumed finding
  - This is an MBA management/organisational study, NOT a psychology study
  - TPS is supra-organisational — studying it cross-firm is methodologically necessary
