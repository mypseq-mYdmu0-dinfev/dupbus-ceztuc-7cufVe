## W5.1 — Qualitative Research and Analysis

### What Qualitative Is
- Explores meaning, lived experience, behaviour, motivations, perceptions — things too complex to reduce to numbers
- NOT easier than quant; just different; still requires rigorous systematic design
- Interpretivist philosophy; inductive reasoning

### Interview Types
- **Structured** = questionnaire read aloud; rigid; used for multiple interviewers; deductive
- **Semi-structured** = preset schedule but flexible; probe with follow-up; feels like conversation; **most suitable for MBA projects**
- **Unstructured** = only broad themes; participant-led; fully inductive; requires experienced interviewer
- ⭐ For TPS topic: semi-structured interviews appropriate; allows probing of unexpected dynamics without losing structure

### Kvale's 9 Interview Question Types (1996)
- Introductory → follow-up → probing → specifying → direct → indirect → structuring → silence → interpreting
- ⭐ Use silence deliberately — gives participant space to elaborate; don't rush to fill gaps
- Indirect questions useful for sensitive areas ("What do most people here think about X?") before asking individual's view

### Successful Interviewer Criteria (Kvale + Bryman)
- Knows what they want to find out; stays focused; doesn't go off-tangent
- Critical when needed — challenge inconsistencies
- Listens actively; interprets without imposing meaning
- Ethically sensitive — participant understands purpose, confidentiality assured
- ⚠️ Power and positionality matter: senior ppts (elites) require different approach; check literature on this

### Focus Groups
- 5`~`10 ppts; group dynamics generate richer discussion than individual interviews
- Use when: group interaction itself is informative; topic involves collective experience
- Risk: dominant participants suppress others; social desirability bias
- ⭐ For TPS topic: focus groups could surface group dynamics directly but risk participants not speaking honestly about TPS behaviour in a group setting — interviews safer for this topic

### Investigative Workshops
- Collaborative problem-solving event (Van Aken et al. 2018)
- Generates rich data via brainstorming, discussion, prototyping
- Related: action research, participatory research, design thinking, co-creation
- ⚠️ Useful alternative to interviews if participants from one org can be convened; less applicable to multi-org cross-firm design

## W5.2 — Qualitative Data Analysis

### Stages of Analysis (Miles & Huberman 1994)
1. **Data reduction** — code, summarise, discard irrelevant; this IS analysis, not prep
2. **Data display** — organise reduced data into accessible compressed form
3. **Conclusions** — draw and verify; initial conclusions are provisional; verify against data and possibly further interviews

### ⭐ Thematic Analysis — Braun & Clarke (2006) — 6 Steps
1. Familiarise with data (read + re-read all transcripts; make initial notes)
2. Generate initial codes (reduce data to meaningful chunks; some codes pre-set from lit; new ones emerge)
3. Search for themes (group codes into broader themes; use "miscellaneous" for outliers)
4. Review themes (check themes make sense; avoid overlap; check sub-themes)
5. Define themes (identify "essence" of each theme; how they interact with aim)
6. Write-up (let participant voice come through via quotes; quotes in italics + quotation marks; 1`~`4 sentences per quote; follow each quote with analyst's interpretation)
- ⚠️ Codes pre-set from lit review are expected; additional codes emerge during analysis — both are normal

### NVivo
- Available at UoL; useful for large volumes
- 📌 **Manual coding equally acceptable; no penalty for not using NVivo** (confirmed by Enis)
- ⭐ If using any software: only UoL-sanctioned tools; this applies to analysis and transcription

### 📌 Transcription Rule (module-specific, non-negotiable)
- **Only permitted: MS Teams / MS Office / MS Copilot**
- Zoom, Otter, Rev, or any third-party = **breach of research integrity** regardless of ethics approval
- Must plan interview recording/transcription workflow around this before data collection begins

### Trustworthiness (Guba & Lincoln 1994) — replaces reliability/validity for qual
- **Credibility** = findings believable and authentic; respondent validation; reflexivity
- **Transferability** = extent findings could apply in other contexts; provide dense description of sample
- **Dependability** = audit trail; document all decisions in research process
- **Confirmability** = researcher acted in good faith; minimised personal value influence; self-reflection notes post-interview
- ⭐ For TPS topic: Culous' TP identity = reflexivity material for confirmability; must acknowledge and manage in methodology chapter

### Mixed Methods
- Technically possible (concurrent Qual+Quant; sequential Quan→Qual; sequential Qual→Quan)
- 📌 **Module directive: avoid mixed methods** — insufficient time; supervisor will almost certainly push back unless very strong justification
- Descriptive stats to set background = NOT mixed methods (this is fine and encouraged)
