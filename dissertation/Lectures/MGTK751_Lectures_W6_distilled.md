## W6.1 — Crafting Recommendations and Conclusions

### What a Recommendation Is
- Specific, actionable advice based on analysis/evidence; not generic; helps recipient solve a problem or improve performance
- ⭐ **Must be SMART** (specific, measurable, achievable/action-oriented, relevant/realistic, time-bound)
- Mix of short, medium, and long-term recommendations expected in A3

### 5-Step Process for Crafting Recommendations
1. Define the problem clearly
2. Analyse the data
3. Consider alternatives (pros/cons of each solution)
4. Prioritise (most likely to be effective + feasible)
5. Communicate clearly (concise language; numbered; reasons explained)

### Conclusions vs. Recommendations
- **Conclusions** = "what" — summary of key findings; what you found and why it matters
- **Recommendations** = "so what" — specific actions based on those findings; the practical output
- Both must be developed together; conclusions provide the foundation; recs act on them
- ⚠️ Poor recommendations = wasted research; key risk is being too generic or not evidenced

### Organisational Research Impact
- Positive: improved decision-making, efficiency, employee satisfaction, innovation
- Negative: resistance to change, inaccurate findings leading to poor decisions, unintended consequences
- ⭐ For TPS topic: acknowledge in A3 that recommendations may face resistance (mgrs may not recognise TPS; culture may resist naming it) — this is a legitimate limitation and a research impact consideration

## W6.2 — Final Report, Presentation and Storytelling

### Full Research Process Reminder
Describing problem → reviewing lit → identifying purpose → choosing design/method → analysing data → interpreting → evaluating → loop back

### Final Report Structure (A3)
- Title page, Declaration, Acknowledgements (optional), Executive Summary (≤350w, in word count), TOC, List of Tables/Figures
- Ch1 Introduction (~800w): background, aims/objectives/RQs, importance, chapter structure
- Ch2 Literature Review (~2,500w): critical review, gaps, conceptual framework at end
- Ch3 Methodology (~850w): approach, data collection (incl. pilot), sampling, analysis, trustworthiness, ethics
- Ch4 Findings (~1,250w): present data (may merge w/ Ch5 for qual thematic analysis)
- Ch5 Analysis/Discussion (~1,250w): relate findings to lit; compare/contrast; update conceptual framework
- Ch6 Conclusions & Recs (~1,000w): SMART recs, limitations, future research, personal reflections
- References (excl. word count), Appendices (excl. word count; include participant info sheet, consent form, ethics approval, interview schedule, sample transcript, coding examples)
- ⭐ Word counts are advisory except Executive Summary (≤350w hard limit)
- Format: Times New Roman or Arial 11`~`12pt; 1.5 line spacing; Harvard referencing throughout

### A2 Presentation Structure (Minto Applied)
- ⭐ **Audience = senior management of client org** (not academics); managerial tone; still Harvard referenced
- Welcome (identify the client) → purpose/importance → methods credibility → key findings → recommendations → call to action → reference list
- ⭐ Must submit as both **.pptx** (auto-submitted to Turnitin) AND **.mp4**
- 📌 Max 10 minutes; penalties for exceeding
- Minto structure for slides: Slide 1 (key message: Situation → Complication → Question → Answer) → Supporting argument slides → Data slides → Conclusion

### Storytelling
- Powerful tool for communicating complex findings to non-academic audiences
- Build rapport, communicate values, facilitate change, enhance learning
- ⭐ For A2: frame TPS as a business risk story, not a sociological phenomenon; make mgrs the heroes who can act on your findings
- Must be authentic, ethically grounded, audience-relevant
- Storytelling = career asset too; you'll narrate this research in job interviews and on your CV
