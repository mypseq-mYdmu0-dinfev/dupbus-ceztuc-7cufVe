## W1.2 — Business Research and Ethics

- ⭐ **Research philosophy governs everything downstream** — ontology → epistemology → methodology → method; must be internally consistent or work fails
- Two main stances (treat as a spectrum, not binary):
  - **Positivist:** objective reality; scientific facts; quantitative; hypothesis testing; researcher independent of subject
  - **Interpretivist:** subjective reality; people define their own realities; qualitative; exploring feelings/perceptions/experiences
- Pragmatism = third option; multiple realities; uses what works
- 📌 **Ethics — module rule:** stay within standard ethics approval; you do not have time for LOREC (high-risk committee) within 36 weeks → effectively a topic-change signal if triggered
- Ethics hard exclusions: vulnerable groups, deception, sensitive topics (bereavement, criminal, embarrassing/personal), studies causing discomfort/anxiety, participants unable to refuse
- ⚠️ Philosophy consistency: "You cannot start with realism and positivism and then want to conduct interviews" — the whole research design must align

## W1.3 — Forming a Research Aim and Objectives

- ⭐ **Evaluative research type = most likely for MBA work-based project** (evaluating effectiveness of policies, programmes, practices — not just describing what exists)
- Research types: descriptive/exploratory (what exists?) → explanatory (why/how does it happen?) → evaluative (how effective is it?)
- ⭐ **Qualitative RQ rule: must start with "what" or "how" — NEVER "why"** (why implies cause and effect; that's quant territory)
- Qualitative approach = most common for MBA projects because: you're working with people; people see the world differently; decisions aren't always rational; we never have perfect knowledge
- **Golden thread (non-negotiable):** Aim → objectives → lit review → RQs → data collection → findings → recommendations → conclusions; everything must trace back
- RQs at proposal stage = provisional; refined/rewritten after lit review — this is expected, not a problem
- Aim = brief statement of overall purpose; objectives = 3`~`4 steps to achieve aim; RQs = 1`~`2 questions derived from lit review (NOT a rephrasing of objectives)
- Topic selection checklist (Saunders): Appropriateness (fits module + has fresh insight potential), Capability (resources, skills, data access), Fulfilment (sustains interest, career relevance)
- ⭐ **For TPS topic:** challenge is pre-identified (you've experienced it); topic sustains genuine interest; evaluative framing natural ("how effective are managerial responses to TP dynamics?")
