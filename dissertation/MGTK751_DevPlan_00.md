## Key Learnings & Standing Rules

**Single organisation vs. multi-org (confirmed Wk3 seminar):**
- Primary expectation = single org; industry-wide permitted with strong rationale in intro
- Multi-org 1-per-org design = no org-level consent letter needed; must be explained in proposal
- Startup framing = for data access problems only; not applicable to TPS (inherently cross-org)

**Mixed methods (module-level directive, W5.2 lecture):**
- Officially advised against for MBA project; not a hard rule for ideation but should be designed out during convergence where viable

**Qualitative as module default (W1.3 lecture):**
- Most MBA projects expected to be interpretivist/qualitative; quant remains valid but requires stronger methodology justification

**Cause and effect — qual vs. quant (#25):**
- Qual: cannot imply causation; no arrows in conceptual framework; no "why" RQs; no words like "impact", "measure", "assess", "effect"
- Quant: hypotheses, arrows, statistical analysis appropriate
- Applies to all future RQ drafting, interview guide design, and framework visualisation

**Qualitative RQ framing rule (W1.3 lecture):**
- RQs must start with "what" or "how" — never "why"
- 1 central RQ + 2`~`3 sub-RQs for qual; sub-RQs derived from lit review

**Evaluative framing preference (W1.3 lecture):**
- Evaluative or explanatory research types preferred over purely exploratory for MBA projects
- When narrowing to selected topics, reframe using "how effective is X?" or "to what extent does Y achieve Z?" as default lens

**Research philosophy consistency (non-negotiable, W1.2 lecture):**
- Ontology → epistemology → methodology → method must be internally consistent
- Cannot combine interpretivist stance with quantitative instruments without strong justification

**Transcription constraint (official files + seminar):**
- Only MS Teams / MS Office / MS Copilot permitted
- Zoom or any other platform = breach of research integrity
