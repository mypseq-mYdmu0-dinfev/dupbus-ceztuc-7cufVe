1. **Topic**
- 1.1. Title — "Tall Poppy Syndrome as a Management Challenge in Knowledge-Based Firms"
- 1.2. One-liner — How TPS dynamics create team cohesion and KM governance risks in KBFs, and what mgrs can do about it
- 1.3. Cross-firm KBF sampling — TPS is supra-organisational; studying it in one firm risks conflating findings w/ that firm's idiosyncratic culture; cross-firm sampling = methodological necessity, not a workaround
- 1.4. Researcher positionality — Culous is a TP; first-hand experience provides reflexivity material for methodology chapter (strength, not conflict)
- 1.5. Research vehicle — CYC; MCF specialising in KM; boutique/specialist tier; Culous as founding chief consultant; simulated status in A1R footnote only (not A2/A3)

2. **Management Challenge**
- 2.1. Primary — Challenge A: team cohesion erosion + KM governance risk
  - When TP's output far exceeds team norm, peers disengage, collaboration declines, morale suffers
  - TP marginalised socially → logically withdraws from KM → converts interpersonal tension into boardroom-level KM governance problem (individual vs. collective success)
  - Mgr dilemma: penalising excellence vs. permitting mediocrity; no clean solution → research value
  - This is NOT a psychological study; focus is on organisational consequence and managerial response
- 2.2. Secondary — Challenge B: TP attrition cost
  - Sustained social exclusion → TP disengages/leaves → measurable HR/financial cost
  - Quantifiable angle; useful supplement in A2/A3 even if A is primary
- 2.3. Tertiary — Challenge C: systemic conditions enabling TPS
  - Reward structures, leadership modelling, cultural norms that allow TPS to persist
  - Boardroom-level cultural problem; elevates scope
  - Too broad for single-researcher project as primary; better as discussion/recommendation layer
- 2.4. Excluded — Challenge D: TP personal wellbeing; ethics risk (sensitive/distress territory)

3. **Why Original**
- 3.1. TPS literature dominated by cultural psychology (AU, NZ focus) and sociological context; management application in KBFs largely absent
- 3.2. Most org behaviour research on TPs assumes net-positive outcome; hidden costs to team and KM are underexplored
- 3.3. KM angle distinguishes this from HR/psychology territory and aligns w/ KBV literature and Lu's PhD in knowledge and information management
- 3.4. Timeless problem (unlike AI-specific topics); findings won't expire

4. **Research Perspective & Participant Rationale**
- 4.1. Primary subjects — TPPs (non-mgr workers in KBFs who've worked alongside a TP and experienced TPS dynamics)
  - Rationale — TPPs are proximal experiencers of TPS; their accounts surface root causes and day-to-day blockers invisible to mgrs; this is fuelling practitioners, not competing w/ them
  - TPPs also more willing to speak (workplace hierarchy doesn't apply cross-firm, and anonymity is protected)
- 4.2. Supplementary — TP + mgr as triangulation/member-checking if secured; NOT to be pre-committed in proposal; present as bonus if secured before analysis
- 4.3. Recruitment criterion — experiential: "worked alongside a standout performer and experienced friction or tension in that context"
  - Note: recruited TPPs may be TPs themselves (high achievers know high achievers); this enriches rather than contaminates data; 2 question guides to accommodate both
- 4.4. Stakeholder matrix

| Stakeholder | Role | Interview guide | Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| TPP | Primary analysis | Guide A | Yes (6 min.) |
| TP | Triangulation | Guide B | Bonus if secured |
| Mgr | Triangulation | Guide C | Bonus if secured |

5. **Methodology**
- 5.1. Research method — explanatory multi-case study (Yin, 2003; Zainal, 2007); each org = one case; 6 orgs = 6 cases; Q confirmed
- 5.2. Data collection — semi-structured interviews (data collection technique within case study design); 6 TPPs default; expand if TP + mgr secured
- 5.3. Analysis — thematic analysis (Braun & Clarke, 2006); within-case first → cross-case synthesis
- 5.4. Multi-org design — 1 ppt per org = no org-level consent needed; isolation rationale in §3.3; consent/feasibility angle in §3.6 only (not A2/A3)
- 5.5. Philosophy — interpretivist; inductive; no cause-and-effect language; RQs start w/ "what"/"how" only

6. **Feasibility**
- 6.1. Participant access — TPP recruitment via Culous' professional network across 10⁺ countries (consulting, entertainment, FMCG, hospitality, NGOs, govt)
- 6.2. Multi-org = 1-per-org design; no consent bottleneck
- 6.3. Anonymisation — org name anonymised; industry stated (per Enis Wk 2); cross-firm design further protects identity
- 6.4. Dual question guide = standard in qual research; ethics-safe if framed at professional dynamics level (not personal emotional disclosure)

**Research Aim**
> "To investigate how TPS dynamics in KBFs generate team cohesion and knowledge governance challenges, surfacing management implications for practitioners"

**Research Objectives**
- RO1: Critically review existing literature on TPS, team dynamics, and KM in KBFs
- RO2: Explore how TPPs in KBFs perceive and experience TPS dynamics
- RO3: Evaluate the organisational and interpersonal conditions that exacerbate or alleviate TPS
- RO4: Formulate management recommendations addressing TPS-related KM and team cohesion risks
